# EUR/USD

## Definition

EUR/USD is the most traded currency pair in the world, representing approximately 23% of daily FX turnover (over $2 trillion/day). It quotes how many US dollars are needed to buy 1 euro. EUR/USD is the primary expression of transatlantic monetary policy divergence, growth differentials, and capital flow dynamics between the US and the Eurozone.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

EUR/USD is the anchor pair in FX markets. Most other G10 pairs are quoted against USD, so EUR/USD movements ripple across the entire FX complex. For a commodity trader, EUR/USD matters because most commodities are priced in USD. A stronger dollar (lower EUR/USD) creates headwinds for commodity prices globally. Additionally, European energy imports (particularly [[TTF Natural Gas]]) are priced against EUR, so EUR/USD directly affects the cost of European energy procurement and flows back into energy [[Forward Curve]] dynamics.

## Fundamentals

### Drivers of EUR strength (EUR/USD higher)
- ECB hawkish relative to Fed (rate differential narrows or inverts)
- Eurozone growth outperformance vs US
- Current account surplus expansion (Eurozone runs structural surplus)
- Capital inflows to European assets (equity, fixed income)
- USD weakness from US fiscal deterioration or reserve diversification

### Drivers of EUR weakness (EUR/USD lower)
- Fed hawkish relative to ECB (US rate advantage widens)
- US growth outperformance
- European political risk (elections, sovereign debt stress, EU fragmentation)
- Energy crisis increasing Eurozone import bill (negative terms of trade shock)
- Risk off environments (USD safe haven bid)

### Seasonality
Mild seasonal patterns. EUR/USD has historically shown strength in Q1 (European current account surpluses from prior year trade flows) and weakness in Q4 (year end USD demand for global funding). However, macro regime dominates seasonality in major FX pairs.

## Key data and reports

- ECB interest rate decisions and press conferences (6 week cycle)
- FOMC interest rate decisions, dot plot, and minutes
- Eurozone PMI flash estimates (monthly, high impact)
- US Non Farm Payrolls (monthly, first Friday)
- US and Eurozone CPI/inflation data
- ECB and Fed balance sheet data (QE/QT)
- CFTC IMM positioning data: net speculative EUR positions
- Eurozone current account data (ECB, monthly)

## Key spreads and relative value

- **EUR/USD vs 2 year rate differential (US 2Y minus German 2Y):** the single strongest short term driver. Rate diff widens in favor of US = EUR/USD lower.
- **EUR/GBP cross:** expresses views on UK vs Eurozone specifically, removing the USD component.
- **EUR/JPY cross:** risk sentiment expression. EUR/JPY falls in risk off (JPY safe haven + EUR risk proxy).
- **EUR/CHF:** European stress gauge. Falls during Eurozone sovereign stress.
- **DXY (Dollar Index):** EUR is 57.6% of DXY weighting, so EUR/USD and DXY are nearly mirror images.

## Important relationships

- Strong inverse correlation with US 2 year yields
- Positive correlation with Eurozone/US growth differential (PMI spread)
- Inverse correlation with crude oil during European energy crises (higher energy costs weaken EUR terms of trade)
- Correlation with equity risk sentiment (EUR mildly risk on, USD mildly risk off in most regimes)
- EUR/USD direction influences commodity prices broadly through the dollar channel
