# EUR/USD

## Definition

EUR/USD is the most traded currency pair in the world, accounting for ~23% of daily FX turnover (over $2 trillion/day). It quotes how many US dollars buy 1 euro. EUR/USD is the primary expression of transatlantic monetary policy divergence, growth differentials, and capital flow dynamics between the US and Eurozone.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

EUR/USD is the anchor pair. Most other G10 pairs are quoted against USD, so EUR/USD movements ripple across the FX complex. For a commodity trader, EUR/USD matters because most commodities are priced in USD. A stronger dollar (lower EUR/USD) is a headwind for global commodity prices. European energy imports (particularly [[TTF Natural Gas]]) are priced in EUR, so EUR/USD directly affects the cost of European energy procurement and feeds back into [[Forward Curve]] dynamics.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Thursday 14:15 CET, ECB rate decision day. Lagarde guides toward a June cut more dovishly than expected and revises 2026 inflation forecasts down 30 bps. EURUSD was 1.0850 going in. Trader was short EUR 5M from 1.0855 sized against the dovish risk. Spot drops to 1.0720 within 40 minutes as US 2Y minus German 2Y widens 12 bps in favor of the dollar. Trader covers EUR 3M at 1.0735 and EUR 2M at 1.0710. P&L: (1.0855 minus 1.0727 average) × 5,000,000 = roughly $6,400 (128 pips, $10 per pip per million). EURJPY fell in sympathy and the [[DXY]] rallied 0.6%.

**Simplified:** EURUSD is how many US dollars buy 1 euro. It is the most traded currency pair on earth. It moves on the difference between Fed and ECB policy, growth and inflation surprises on either side of the Atlantic, and risk sentiment. Traders watch ECB and FOMC meetings, CPI prints, and PMI data. In FX one pip is 0.0001, and on a standard 100K EUR lot that equals $10. A 100 pip move is $1,000 per lot.

## Fundamentals

### Drivers of EUR strength (EUR/USD higher)
- ECB hawkish relative to Fed (rate differential narrows or inverts)
- Eurozone growth outperformance vs US
- Current account surplus expansion (Eurozone runs structural surplus)
- Capital inflows to European assets (equity, fixed income)
- USD weakness from US fiscal deterioration or reserve diversification

### Drivers of EUR weakness (EUR/USD lower)
- Fed hawkish relative to ECB (US rate advantage widens)
- US growth outperformance
- European political risk (elections, sovereign debt stress, EU fragmentation)
- Energy crisis raising Eurozone import bill (negative terms of trade shock)
- Risk off environments (USD safe haven bid)

### Seasonality
Mild seasonal patterns. EUR/USD has historically shown strength in Q1 (European current account surpluses from prior year trade flows) and weakness in Q4 (year end USD demand for global funding). Macro regime dominates seasonality in major FX pairs.

## Key data and reports

- ECB interest rate decisions and press conferences (6 week cycle)
- FOMC interest rate decisions, dot plot, and minutes
- Eurozone PMI flash estimates (monthly, high impact)
- US Non Farm Payrolls (monthly, first Friday)
- US and Eurozone CPI/inflation data
- ECB and Fed balance sheet data (QE/QT)
- CFTC IMM positioning: net speculative EUR positions
- Eurozone current account data (ECB, monthly)

## Key spreads and relative value

- **EUR/USD vs 2 year rate differential (US 2Y minus German 2Y):** the strongest short term driver. Rate diff widens in favor of US = EUR/USD lower.
- **EUR/GBP cross:** expresses views on UK vs Eurozone, removing the USD component.
- **EUR/JPY cross:** risk sentiment expression. Falls in risk off (JPY safe haven + EUR risk proxy).
- **EUR/CHF:** European stress gauge. Falls during Eurozone sovereign stress.
- **DXY (Dollar Index):** EUR is 57.6% of DXY weighting, so EUR/USD and DXY are nearly mirror images.

## Important relationships

- Strong inverse correlation with US 2 year yields
- Positive correlation with Eurozone/US growth differential (PMI spread)
- Inverse correlation with crude oil during European energy crises (higher energy costs weaken EUR terms of trade)
- Correlation with equity risk sentiment (EUR mildly risk on, USD mildly risk off in most regimes)
- EUR/USD direction influences commodity prices broadly through the dollar channel
