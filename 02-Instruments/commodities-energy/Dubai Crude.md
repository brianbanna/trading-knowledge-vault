# Dubai Crude

## Definition

Dubai is the pricing benchmark for medium sour crude flowing from the Middle East to Asia. It is a blend of Dubai and Oman grades: 31 API, 2% sulfur, typical of Persian Gulf exports. Unlike [[Brent Crude]] (light sweet, North Sea seaborne) or [[WTI Crude Oil]] (landlocked, Cushing delivered), Dubai prices through a Platts managed OTC swaps market and trades as a deliverable forward on the Dubai Mercantile Exchange (DME). It is the reference for about 2/3 of all crude sold to Asia, including Saudi Aramco's Official Selling Prices (OSPs).

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Any crude flowing East of Suez references Dubai. Saudi Aramco, Kuwait, Iraq, and UAE set Asian export prices as differentials to Dubai/Oman. The [[EFS]] (Exchange of Futures for Swaps) spread links Brent futures to Dubai swaps, bridging Atlantic Basin and Asian crude pricing. Strong Asian demand vs Atlantic narrows the Brent/Dubai spread. Atlantic tightening widens it. Dubai is essential for Middle East to Asia crude flows, Asian refining margins, and cross basin [[Location Arbitrage]].

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Chinese refinery runs surge in Q1 2026, pulling Middle East barrels East. Dubai swaps for March delivery sit at $78/bbl, [[Brent Crude]] March futures at $82/bbl, [[EFS]] at $4/bbl. Trader buys Dubai swaps, sells Brent futures, targeting EFS compression. EFS narrows to $2.50: profit $1.50/bbl. Reverse scenario: surprise OPEC+ output increase and Asian demand disappointment widen EFS to $5.50: loss $1.50/bbl.

**Simplified:** Dubai is the Asian benchmark, Brent is the European benchmark. The gap between them (EFS) reflects which side of the world is tighter. Bet on the gap closing if Asia is heating up, widening if Europe is.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Dubai swap pricing:** Platts' Market on Close (MOC) assessment of partials (25,000 barrel cargoes) traded in the [[Platts Window]].

**EFS (Exchange of Futures for Swaps):** `EFS = Brent Futures Price - Dubai Swap Price`

**OSP formula (Saudi Aramco to Asia):** `Selling Price = Dubai/Oman Average + Aramco Differential`

The Aramco differential is reset monthly and is one of the most watched pricing signals in global oil.

## Common misconceptions

**"Dubai is just another grade like Brent or WTI."** Different market structure. Dubai trades as OTC swaps and forwards rather than exchange listed futures (DME lists an Oman contract). Pricing relies on Platts assessments, not continuous exchange trading.

**"Dubai only matters for Middle East crude."** Dubai pricing affects Asian refining margins, Asian fuel oil markets, and commodity currencies linked to Asian demand (AUD, IDR). It is the demand side benchmark for the fastest growing oil consuming region.

**"The EFS is stable."** Brent/Dubai swings on Atlantic vs Pacific Basin dynamics, seasonal refinery demand, and OPEC allocation. Actively traded as relative value.
