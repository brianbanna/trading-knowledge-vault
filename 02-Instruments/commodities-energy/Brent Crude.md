# Brent Crude

## Overview

Brent is the global seaborne crude benchmark, pricing about 2/3 of internationally traded crude. Originally named after a single North Sea field, the benchmark is now a basket of 5 grades (BFOET: Brent, Forties, Oseberg, Ekofisk, Troll). Light sweet: 38 API, 0.4% sulfur. Trades on ICE Futures Europe and as a cash settled contract on CME (BZ). Troll joined the basket in 2023. Platts is evaluating WTI Midland delivered into Rotterdam to preserve liquidity as North Sea output declines.

## Fundamentals

### Supply Drivers
- OPEC+ production decisions (Saudi Arabia, Russia, UAE swing producers)
- Non OPEC growth: US shale, Brazil pre salt, Guyana
- North Sea decline (BFOET redesigned to compensate)
- Geopolitical disruptions: Middle East, Russia, Libya, Nigeria, Venezuela
- Strategic Petroleum Reserve (SPR) releases

### Demand Drivers
- Global refinery throughput and margins ([[Crack Spread]])
- Chinese and Indian imports (marginal barrels)
- Seasonal: driving season (Q2/Q3), winter heating
- Jet fuel recovery cycles
- Petrochemical feedstock (naphtha cracking)

### Seasonality
Q2 strongest (refinery turnaround ends, summer driving ramps). Q4 demand dip before winter heating offsets. OPEC meetings (Jun and Nov/Dec) create event risk. Turnaround season (Mar to May, Sep to Oct) reduces crude demand.

## Curve Structure
Brent oscillates between [[Contango]] and [[Backwardation]] depending on supply/demand balance. Backwardation appears during tightness (OPEC cuts, geopolitical disruptions, low inventories). Contango appears during oversupply (demand destruction, OPEC price wars, inventory builds). The M1 to M6 time spread is the standard tightness gauge. Steep backwardation (>$5 M1 to M6) signals acute tightness. [[Convenience Yield]] dominates the front during draws.

## Key Spreads and Relative Value
- [[Brent-WTI Spread]]: most traded inter benchmark crude spread. Reflects Atlantic Basin economics, US export capacity, relative supply/demand.
- Brent/Dubai spread (EFS, Exchange of Futures for Swaps): East vs West crude pricing. Wider = Atlantic tighter than Asia.
- [[Crack Spread]] (Brent based): Brent vs gasoil and gasoline. European refining margins.
- [[Calendar Spread]]: M1 vs M2, M1 vs M6, Dec/Dec annuals. Core relative value trades.
- DFL (Dated to Frontline): spread between Dated Brent and front month ICE Brent futures. Signals physical tightness.

## Important Relationships
- Inverse [[Correlation]] with USD (DXY): stronger dollar raises local currency oil cost for importers, dampens demand
- Positive correlation with [[Commodity Currencies]] (CAD, NOK, RUB)
- Drives [[Crack Spread]] economics for European refiners
- OPEC+ decisions are the largest short term supply variable
- Linked to [[Geopolitical Risk]] in Middle East and Russia
