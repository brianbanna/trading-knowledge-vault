# Zinc Futures

## Definition

Zinc is the 4th most consumed metal globally, primarily used for galvanizing steel (~50% of demand) for corrosion protection in construction, infrastructure, and automotive. Also used in alloys (brass, die casting), chemicals, and batteries. Trades on the [[LME]] (25 tonne lots) and [[SHFE]]. Global production is ~14 million tonnes per year, with China, Australia, and Peru as the largest mine producers. Zinc demand is closely tied to steel production and construction, making it a cyclical industrial metal with strong correlation to global manufacturing PMIs.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Zinc demand is a proxy for global steel production and construction activity. When Chinese property construction booms, galvanized steel demand surges and zinc benefits. The [[Lead Futures|lead]]/zinc ratio is a common relative value trade because both metals share supply chains but have different demand drivers. The concentrate market also matters: when mine supply is tight, zinc concentrate TC/RCs fall, squeezing smelter margins and potentially cutting refined zinc output. For macro traders, zinc correlates with Chinese industrial production and global construction.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Chinese property starts recover in early 2026 after government stimulus. Trader goes long LME zinc at $2,600/tonne, expecting galvanizing demand to rise. Over 3 months zinc rallies to $2,900 as construction steel demand pulls galvanized production higher: +$300/tonne. If the stimulus fails to translate into actual construction activity, zinc demand disappoints, inventories build, and zinc drops to $2,400: -$200/tonne.

**Simplified:** Zinc coats steel to prevent rust. Steel goes into buildings. Buildings come from China. Bet on zinc when China's property cycle turns up, fade when it stalls.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**LME contract specifications:**
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Exchange | LME |
| Ticker | ZS |
| Contract size | 25 metric tonnes |
| Quotation | USD per tonne |
| Settlement | Physical delivery (LME warehouse) |

**Demand breakdown:**
- Galvanizing: ~50%
- Alloys (brass, die casting): ~17%
- Chemicals: ~6%
- Rolled zinc: ~6%
- Other: ~21%

**Key ratio:** Lead/Zinc ratio = LME Lead Price / LME Zinc Price (typically 0.75 to 0.95)

## Common misconceptions

**"Zinc is boring because it's mostly galvanizing."** Concentration of demand in a single application makes zinc highly sensitive to construction cycles, creating sharp cyclical moves.

**"Zinc and copper always move together."** They are correlated but diverge based on construction vs electronics/EV demand drivers.

**"Mine supply tightness immediately affects zinc prices."** There is a lag because concentrate must be smelted. Tight mine supply first shows up in TC/RCs before affecting refined metal availability and prices.
