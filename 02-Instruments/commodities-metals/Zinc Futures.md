# Zinc Futures

## Definition

Zinc is the fourth most consumed metal globally, primarily used for galvanizing steel (approximately 50% of demand), which provides corrosion protection for construction, infrastructure, and automotive applications. It is also used in alloys (brass, die casting), chemicals, and batteries. Zinc trades on the [[LME]] (25 tonne lots) and [[SHFE]]. Global production is approximately 14 million tonnes per year, with China, Australia, and Peru as the largest mine producers. Zinc demand is closely tied to steel production and construction activity, making it a cyclical industrial metal with strong correlations to global manufacturing PMIs.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Zinc demand is a proxy for global steel production and construction activity. When Chinese property construction booms, galvanized steel demand surges and zinc benefits. The [[Lead Futures|lead]]/zinc ratio is a common relative value trade because both metals share similar supply chains but have different demand drivers. Zinc's concentrate market is also important: when mine supply is tight, TC/RCs for zinc concentrate fall, squeezing smelter margins and potentially reducing refined zinc output. For macro traders, zinc prices correlate with Chinese industrial production and global construction activity.

## Concrete example

Chinese property starts are recovering in early 2026 after government stimulus. A trader goes long LME zinc at $2,600/tonne, expecting galvanizing demand to increase. Over 3 months, zinc rallies to $2,900 as construction steel demand pulls galvanized steel production higher. Profit: $300/tonne. If instead the property stimulus fails to translate into actual construction activity, zinc demand disappoints, inventories build, and zinc drops to $2,400, losing $200/tonne.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**LME contract specifications:**
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Exchange | LME |
| Ticker | ZS |
| Contract size | 25 metric tonnes |
| Quotation | USD per tonne |
| Settlement | Physical delivery (LME warehouse) |

**Demand breakdown:**
- Galvanizing: ~50%
- Alloys (brass, die casting): ~17%
- Chemicals: ~6%
- Rolled zinc: ~6%
- Other: ~21%

**Key ratio:** Lead/Zinc ratio = LME Lead Price / LME Zinc Price (typically 0.75 to 0.95)

## Common misconceptions

**"Zinc is boring because it's mostly used for galvanizing."** The concentration of demand in a single application makes zinc highly sensitive to construction cycles, creating sharp cyclical moves.

**"Zinc and copper always move together."** They are correlated but can diverge significantly based on construction vs electronics/EV demand drivers.

**"Mine supply tightness immediately affects zinc prices."** There is a lag because concentrate must be smelted. Tight mine supply first shows up in TC/RCs before affecting refined metal availability and prices.
