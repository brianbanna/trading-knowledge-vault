# Aluminum Futures

## Definition

Aluminum is the most traded base metal on the [[LME]] by volume and the most widely used non ferrous metal globally. Prized for light weight, corrosion resistance, and conductivity, with primary uses in transport (automotive, aerospace), packaging (cans, foil), construction (facades, window frames), and electrical (power cables). Smelting requires ~14,000 kWh per tonne, making electricity the single largest cost input (30 to 40% of production cost). China dominates production at ~60% of output, and marginal cost is largely set by Chinese electricity prices and environmental policy.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Aluminum is a proxy for global energy and industrial policy. Because production costs are dominated by electricity, prices are sensitive to energy shocks, carbon pricing, and Chinese power market reforms. When Chinese authorities curtail smelting for emissions targets (2021), supply contracts sharply. Recycled aluminum requires only 5% of primary energy, creating a scrap/primary price ratio that caps upside. For cross asset traders, aluminum correlates with Chinese industrial production, European energy costs, and AUD.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Trader expects European TTF gas to spike in Q4 2025, lifting European smelter costs above $2,800/tonne. LME aluminum at $2,400 puts European smelters at curtailment risk. She goes long LME aluminum at $2,400. If TTF spikes and 1 million tonnes of European capacity curtails, LME aluminum could rally to $2,700: +$300/tonne. If TTF falls and smelters keep running, supply stays ample and aluminum drops to $2,250: -$150/tonne.

**Simplified:** Aluminum costs electricity to make. Expensive power means expensive aluminum. If you expect energy prices to rise, you expect aluminum to rise, because the marginal smelter shuts down.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**LME contract specifications:**
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Exchange | LME |
| Ticker | AH |
| Contract size | 25 metric tonnes |
| Quotation | USD per tonne |
| Settlement | Physical delivery (LME warehouse) |
| Prompt dates | Daily to 3 months, monthly to 63 months |

**Production cost breakdown:**
- Electricity: 30 to 40%
- Alumina: 30 to 35%
- Other (carbon, labor, overhead): 25 to 35%

**Energy conversion:** ~14,000 kWh per tonne. At $0.05/kWh = $700 energy cost. At $0.10/kWh = $1,400.

## Common misconceptions

**"Aluminum is just a base metal like copper."** Aluminum's production economics are dominated by electricity, not mine output. Trading aluminum requires understanding energy markets.

**"China's 60% share means Chinese policy is the only driver."** European and Middle Eastern smelter economics, Russian supply disruptions, and Indian demand growth all affect the balance.

**"LME aluminum is what end users pay."** End users pay LME + regional premium + conversion premium. All in cost runs 15 to 25% above LME.
