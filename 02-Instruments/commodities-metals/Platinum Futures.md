# Platinum Futures

## Definition

Platinum is a rare precious metal with critical industrial uses: catalytic converters for diesel vehicles (~40% of demand), jewelry (~25%), and increasingly hydrogen fuel cells (PEM electrolyzers). Supply is concentrated: South Africa produces ~70% of global output, Russia ~12%, Zimbabwe ~8%. Trades on [[COMEX]] (CME Group, PL, 50 troy oz contracts) and [[LBMA]] OTC. Historically platinum traded at a premium to [[Gold Futures|gold]], but since 2015 it has run at a persistent and unusual discount, reflecting the decline of diesel in Europe and weak jewelry demand.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

The platinum discount to gold is historically anomalous and creates long term relative value opportunities. The bull case rests on hydrogen fuel cells: green hydrogen production via PEM electrolyzers requires 30 to 60 grams of platinum per MW of capacity. Under IEA hydrogen scenarios, platinum demand could grow 500,000 to 1,000,000 oz per year (from a ~8 million oz market). South African concentration means power outages (Eskom load shedding), labor strikes, and political instability create recurring disruptions. For FX traders, platinum prices affect ZAR through mining revenue and exports.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Trader goes long platinum at $950/oz and short gold at $2,400/oz, betting on the spread narrowing. Platinum sits at a 60% discount to gold (historical average near parity). If hydrogen fuel cell demand accelerates and South African supply disappoints, platinum rallies to $1,300 while gold stays at $2,400, narrowing the discount to 46%: +$350/oz on the platinum leg. If diesel bans accelerate and catalytic demand collapses faster than fuel cells ramp, platinum drops to $800: -$150/oz.

**Simplified:** Platinum used to be more expensive than gold. Now it is much cheaper. The bet is that hydrogen demand and South African supply problems will pull platinum back up toward gold. The risk: diesel cars die before hydrogen scales up.

## Common misconceptions

**"Platinum is just a smaller version of gold."** Platinum has fundamentally different demand drivers (industrial vs investment) and supply dynamics (concentrated vs diversified).

**"Diesel decline kills platinum."** Substitution into hydrogen fuel cells and continued heavy duty diesel use (trucks, ships) partially offset the light vehicle diesel decline.

**"Platinum and palladium are interchangeable."** Both are used in catalytic converters, but switching requires significant R&D by automakers and takes 12 to 18 months to implement.
