# Platinum Futures

## Definition

Platinum is a rare precious metal with critical industrial applications, primarily in catalytic converters for diesel vehicles (~40% of demand), jewelry (~25%), and increasingly in hydrogen fuel cells (proton exchange membrane electrolyzers). Supply is extremely concentrated: South Africa produces approximately 70% of global output, with Russia (~12%) and Zimbabwe (~8%) contributing most of the remainder. Platinum trades on [[COMEX]] (CME Group, ticker PL, 50 troy ounce contracts) and the [[LBMA]] OTC market. Historically, platinum traded at a premium to [[Gold Futures|gold]], but since 2015 it has traded at a persistent and unusual discount, reflecting the decline of diesel vehicles in Europe and weak jewelry demand.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

The platinum discount to gold is historically anomalous and creates long term relative value opportunities. The bull case for platinum rests on hydrogen fuel cell demand: green hydrogen production using PEM electrolyzers requires 30 to 60 grams of platinum per MW of capacity. If hydrogen adoption accelerates per IEA scenarios, platinum demand could increase by 500,000 to 1,000,000 ounces per year (from a total market of ~8 million ounces). South African supply concentration means that power outages (Eskom load shedding), labor strikes, and political instability create recurring supply disruptions. For FX traders, platinum prices affect the South African rand (ZAR) through mining revenue and export channels.

## Concrete example

A trader goes long platinum at $950/oz and short gold at $2,400/oz, betting on the platinum/gold spread narrowing. Platinum is at a 60% discount to gold (historically, the average was near parity). If hydrogen fuel cell demand accelerates and South African supply disappoints, platinum could rally to $1,300 while gold stays at $2,400, narrowing the discount to 46% and earning $350/oz on the platinum leg. If diesel bans accelerate and catalytic converter demand collapses faster than fuel cells ramp, platinum could fall to $800, widening the discount and losing $150/oz.

## Common misconceptions

**"Platinum is just a smaller version of gold."** Platinum has fundamentally different demand drivers (industrial vs investment) and supply dynamics (concentrated vs diversified).

**"The diesel decline kills platinum."** Substitution into hydrogen fuel cells and continued heavy duty diesel use (trucks, ships) partially offset the light vehicle diesel decline.

**"Platinum and palladium are interchangeable."** While both are used in catalytic converters, switching between them requires significant R&D investment by automakers and takes 12 to 18 months to implement.
