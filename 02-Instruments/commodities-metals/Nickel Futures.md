# Nickel Futures

## Definition

Nickel is one of the most volatile base metals, used primarily in stainless steel (~70% of demand) and increasingly in EV batteries (nickel sulfate for lithium ion cathodes). Trades on the [[LME]] (6 tonne lots) and [[SHFE]]. The critical market distinction is Class 1 nickel (refined, battery grade, >99.8% purity, LME deliverable) vs Class 2 nickel (ferronickel and nickel pig iron, used in stainless steel, not LME deliverable). Indonesia dominates mine production (~50%) and has reshaped the market through massive nickel pig iron (NPI) expansion. The 2022 LME nickel squeeze, which spiked prices to $100,000/tonne before the exchange cancelled trades, exposed the market's structural fragility.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Nickel sits at the intersection of 2 mega trends: stainless steel demand (China construction and infrastructure) and EV battery demand (energy transition). The Class 1/Class 2 split creates pricing dislocations: LME nickel (Class 1) can trade at a significant premium or discount to NPI/ferronickel. Indonesian HPAL (High Pressure Acid Leaching) plants are attempting to bridge this gap by converting laterite ore into battery grade nickel, but the technology is still scaling. Nickel's volatility and Class 1/Class 2 dynamics create both outright and relative value opportunities.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Trader expects European EV sales to accelerate, tightening Class 1 supply for battery production. LME nickel at $16,000/tonne. She goes long, targeting $19,000. If European EV mandates tighten and battery producers scramble for Class 1, LME nickel rallies to $20,000: +$4,000/tonne. If Indonesian HPAL output ramps faster than expected and floods Class 1 supply, nickel falls to $14,000: -$2,000/tonne.

**Simplified:** Two nickel markets in one: dirty nickel for steel, clean nickel for batteries. LME only prices the clean kind. EV demand needs the clean kind. Bet on clean nickel scarcity if EVs accelerate and Indonesian battery grade supply lags.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**LME contract specifications:**
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Exchange | LME |
| Ticker | NI |
| Contract size | 6 metric tonnes |
| Quotation | USD per tonne |
| Settlement | Physical delivery (LME warehouse) |

**Nickel classes:**
- Class 1: >99.8% Ni, LME deliverable (cathode, briquettes, rounds)
- Class 2: ferronickel (20 to 40% Ni), NPI (8 to 15% Ni), not LME deliverable

**Key supply sources:**
- Indonesia: ~50% of mine production (mostly NPI/ferronickel)
- Philippines: ~10%
- Russia (Nornickel): ~6%, Class 1
- New Caledonia, Australia, Canada: smaller shares

## Common misconceptions

**"LME nickel represents the whole nickel market."** LME prices only Class 1, a shrinking share of global production. Most nickel (NPI, ferronickel) is not LME deliverable.

**"EV demand will send nickel to the moon."** Indonesian NPI capacity has actually crashed nickel prices by creating a Class 2 surplus. Battery demand is growing but so is Indonesian supply.

**"The 2022 squeeze was a one off."** The structural setup (small LME deliverable supply, concentrated shorts) persists. Nickel remains exposed to delivery squeezes.
