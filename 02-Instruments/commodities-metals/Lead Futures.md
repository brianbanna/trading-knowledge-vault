# Lead Futures

## Definition

Lead is a mature base metal with primary demand from lead acid batteries (~85% of consumption), used in vehicle starting/lighting/ignition, uninterruptible power supplies, and telecom backup. Trades on the [[LME]] (25 tonne lots). What sets lead apart is its extraordinary recycling rate: over 95% of lead acid batteries are recycled, making secondary (recycled) lead the dominant supply source in developed markets. China is both largest producer and consumer, ~40% of global refined output.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Lead is the most mature and least volatile major base metal, making it a relative value anchor. The [[Zinc Futures|lead/zinc ratio]] is one of the most commonly traded relative value pairs on the LME because lead and zinc share geological deposits (mined together as polymetallic ores) but have different demand profiles. Lead's high recycling rate creates a built in supply response: rising prices pull more scrap, capping upside. This makes lead less reactive to mine supply disruptions than copper or zinc. For portfolio construction, lead diversifies within the base metals complex.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Trader notices the lead/zinc ratio has fallen to 0.78, near the low end of its 5 year range (0.75 to 0.95). She goes long lead and short zinc, betting on mean reversion. If the ratio reverts to 0.88 over 3 months (lead outperforms zinc by 10%), she profits. If zinc rallies on a Chinese construction boom while lead stays flat, the ratio drops to 0.72 and the spread trade loses.

**Simplified:** Lead and zinc come out of the same mines but go to different customers. When their price ratio drifts far from average, traders bet on it snapping back. Long the cheap one, short the expensive one.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**LME contract specifications:**
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Exchange | LME |
| Ticker | PB |
| Contract size | 25 metric tonnes |
| Quotation | USD per tonne |
| Settlement | Physical delivery (LME warehouse) |

**Demand breakdown:**
- Lead acid batteries: ~85%
- Lead sheet and pipe: ~5%
- Ammunition: ~3%
- Other (chemicals, alloys): ~7%

**Lead/zinc ratio:** `Ratio = LME Lead Price / LME Zinc Price`

**Recycling dynamics:** Secondary lead supply adjusts within 2 to 4 weeks of a price change, one of the fastest supply responses in commodities.

## Common misconceptions

**"Lead demand is declining because of EV adoption."** EVs use lithium ion for traction but lead acid batteries are still required for 12V systems even in EVs, plus backup power and telecom. Demand is stable, not declining.

**"Lead is just a smaller version of copper or zinc."** Lead's scrap dynamics make it fundamentally different. The 95% recycling rate means mine production matters less for lead than for any other base metal.

**"Environmental concerns will eliminate lead use."** Lead acid batteries are the most recycled consumer product in the world. The circular economy for lead is highly developed.
