# Lead Futures

## Definition

Lead is a mature base metal with primary demand from lead acid batteries (~85% of consumption), which are used in vehicles (starting/lighting/ignition), uninterruptible power supplies, and telecommunications backup. It trades on the [[LME]] (25 tonne lots). What makes lead unique among base metals is its extraordinarily high recycling rate: over 95% of lead acid batteries are recycled, making secondary (recycled) lead the dominant supply source in developed markets. China is both the largest producer and consumer, accounting for approximately 40% of global refined lead output.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Lead is the most mature and least volatile of the major base metals, making it a relative value anchor. The [[Zinc Futures|lead/zinc ratio]] is one of the most commonly traded relative value pairs on the LME because lead and zinc share similar geological deposits (often mined together as polymetallic ores) but have very different demand profiles. Lead's high recycling rate creates a built in supply response: when prices rise, scrap collection increases, capping upside. This makes lead less responsive to mine supply disruptions than copper or zinc. For portfolio construction, lead provides diversification within the base metals complex.

## Concrete example

A trader notices the lead/zinc ratio has fallen to 0.78, near the low end of its 5 year range (0.75 to 0.95). She goes long lead and short zinc, expecting mean reversion. If the ratio reverts to 0.88 over 3 months (lead outperforms zinc by 10%), she profits. If instead zinc rallies on a Chinese construction boom while lead remains flat, the ratio could fall further to 0.72, and the spread trade loses.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**LME contract specifications:**
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Exchange | LME |
| Ticker | PB |
| Contract size | 25 metric tonnes |
| Quotation | USD per tonne |
| Settlement | Physical delivery (LME warehouse) |

**Demand breakdown:**
- Lead acid batteries: ~85%
- Lead sheet and pipe: ~5%
- Ammunition: ~3%
- Other (chemicals, alloys): ~7%

**Lead/zinc ratio:** `Ratio = LME Lead Price / LME Zinc Price`

**Recycling dynamics:** Secondary lead supply typically adjusts within 2 to 4 weeks of a price change, making it one of the fastest supply responses in commodities.

## Common misconceptions

**"Lead demand is declining because of EV adoption."** While EVs use lithium ion batteries, lead acid batteries remain essential for 12V systems even in EVs, for backup power, and for telecommunications. Demand is stable, not declining.

**"Lead is just a smaller version of copper or zinc."** Lead's scrap supply dynamics make it fundamentally different. The 95% recycling rate means mine production is less important for lead than for any other base metal.

**"Environmental concerns will eliminate lead use."** Lead acid batteries are the most recycled consumer product in the world. The circular economy for lead is highly developed and efficient.
