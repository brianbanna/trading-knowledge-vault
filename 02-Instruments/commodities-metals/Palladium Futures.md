# Palladium Futures

## Definition

Palladium is a platinum group metal (PGM) used primarily in catalytic converters for gasoline vehicles (~85% of demand). Supply is extremely concentrated: Russia (Nornickel, ~40% of global output) and South Africa (~35%) dominate production. Palladium trades on [[COMEX]] (CME Group, ticker PA, 100 troy ounce contracts) and the [[LBMA]] OTC market. Palladium experienced a dramatic bull market from 2016 to 2022, rallying from $500 to over $3,000/oz as gasoline vehicle emission standards tightened globally and supply could not keep pace. Since then, the rise of electric vehicles (which use no catalytic converters) has shifted the market from persistent deficit to emerging surplus.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Palladium's story is a case study in how structural demand shifts can reverse a multi year trend. The transition from deficit to surplus as EV adoption reduces gasoline vehicle production is the defining fundamental for palladium. Russian supply concentration adds geopolitical risk: sanctions or export restrictions on Nornickel could tighten the market despite the demand headwinds. The palladium/platinum ratio has undergone a dramatic shift: palladium went from a 50% discount to platinum to a 200% premium, and is now normalizing. For traders, palladium offers high volatility and strong thematic plays on the pace of EV adoption.

## Concrete example

A trader believes EV adoption in China and Europe will accelerate faster than consensus, reducing gasoline vehicle production by 5% per year. Palladium is at $1,000/oz. She goes short palladium. If EV sales surge and automakers cut gasoline engine production, catalytic converter demand drops 15% and palladium falls to $700/oz, earning $300/oz. If instead a supply disruption at Nornickel (Russian sanctions escalation) removes 500,000 ounces from the market, palladium could spike to $1,400/oz despite weak demand, losing $400/oz.

**Supply concentration:**
- Russia (Nornickel): ~40%
- South Africa: ~35%
- Other (Canada, US, Zimbabwe): ~25%

**Demand: ~85% autocatalyst (gasoline vehicles)**

**Key metric:** Global gasoline vehicle production × palladium loading per vehicle ≈ palladium demand. As EVs displace gasoline vehicles, this product declines.

## Common misconceptions

**"Palladium is dead because of EVs."** The transition takes decades. Hundreds of millions of gasoline vehicles will still be produced in the next 10 to 15 years, requiring palladium catalytic converters.

**"Russia can be easily replaced as a palladium supplier."** Nornickel's production comes from unique nickel copper PGM ore bodies that are not replicated elsewhere. New mines take 7 to 10 years to develop.

**"Palladium and platinum are perfectly substitutable."** Automakers have begun switching some applications from palladium to platinum, but it requires costly catalyst redesigns and testing. The switch is gradual, not instantaneous.
