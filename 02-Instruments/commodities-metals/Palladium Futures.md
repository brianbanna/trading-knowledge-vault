# Palladium Futures

## Definition

Palladium is a platinum group metal (PGM) used primarily in catalytic converters for gasoline vehicles (~85% of demand). Supply is extremely concentrated: Russia (Nornickel, ~40% of global output) and South Africa (~35%). Trades on [[COMEX]] (CME Group, PA, 100 troy oz contracts) and [[LBMA]] OTC. Palladium ran a dramatic bull market from 2016 to 2022, rallying from $500 to over $3,000/oz as gasoline emission standards tightened globally and supply could not keep pace. EV adoption (no catalytic converters) has since flipped the market from persistent deficit to emerging surplus.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Palladium is a case study in how structural demand shifts reverse multi year trends. The shift from deficit to surplus as EV adoption cuts gasoline vehicle production is the defining fundamental. Russian supply concentration adds geopolitical risk: sanctions or Nornickel export restrictions could tighten the market despite demand headwinds. The palladium/platinum ratio underwent a dramatic shift: palladium went from a 50% discount to platinum to a 200% premium, and is now normalizing. For traders, palladium offers high volatility and a strong thematic play on EV adoption pace.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Trader believes EV adoption in China and Europe will accelerate faster than consensus, cutting gasoline vehicle production 5% per year. Palladium at $1,000/oz. She goes short. If EV sales surge and automakers cut gasoline engine production, catalytic converter demand drops 15% and palladium falls to $700: +$300/oz. If a Nornickel supply disruption (Russian sanctions escalation) removes 500,000 oz from the market, palladium spikes to $1,400 despite weak demand: -$400/oz.

**Simplified:** Palladium goes into gasoline car exhaust scrubbers. EVs do not have exhaust. As more EVs are sold, demand for palladium drops. Bet against palladium if you think EVs are winning faster than the market expects.

- South Africa: ~35%
- Other (Canada, US, Zimbabwe): ~25%

**Key metric:** Global gasoline vehicle production x palladium loading per vehicle ~ palladium demand. As EVs displace gasoline vehicles, this product declines.

## Common misconceptions

**"Palladium is dead because of EVs."** Transition takes decades. Hundreds of millions of gasoline vehicles will still be produced over the next 10 to 15 years, requiring palladium catalysts.

**"Russia can be easily replaced."** Nornickel produces from unique nickel copper PGM ore bodies not replicated elsewhere. New mines take 7 to 10 years to develop.

**"Palladium and platinum are perfectly substitutable."** Automakers have begun switching some applications from palladium to platinum, but it requires costly catalyst redesigns and testing. The switch is gradual.
