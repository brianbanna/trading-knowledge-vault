# LME

## Definition

The London Metal Exchange is the world's premier exchange for base metals, handling over $14 trillion in annual notional. Founded 1877, LME trades 6 primary contracts: [[Copper Futures|copper]], [[Aluminum Futures|aluminum]], [[Zinc Futures|zinc]], [[Nickel Futures|nickel]], [[Lead Futures|lead]], and [[Tin Futures|tin]]. What makes LME unique is its prompt date system: instead of monthly expiry contracts like COMEX or SHFE, LME trades on specific calendar dates (daily out to 3 months, then weekly, then monthly). The 3 month contract is the most liquid. LME also runs a global network of approved warehouses for physical delivery, storage, and withdrawal.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

LME prices are the global reference for industrial metals. Virtually every physical metal contract is priced as a premium or discount to LME. The LME [[Cash to 3 Month Spread]] is the key indicator of physical tightness. [[LME Warehouse Stocks]] and [[Cancelled Warrants]] provide real time inventory signals. For FX traders, LME metals drive [[Commodity Currencies]] like AUD, CLP, and ZMW. The daily prompt date structure creates more granular spread trading opportunities than standard monthly futures.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Metals trader observes LME copper cash at $9,850/tonne while 3M is at $9,780, a $70 backwardation. [[Cancelled Warrants]] rising (physical demand). Buys 10 cash/3M spreads expecting tightness to widen the backwardation. Major Chilean smelter goes offline. Backwardation widens to $150. Gain: $80 × 25 tonnes × 10 = $20,000. Counter scenario: Chinese demand disappoints, metal flows into LME warehouses, spread flips to $30 contango. Loss: $100 × 25 × 10 = $25,000.

**Simplified:** LME is the global pricing hub for industrial metals. Unlike US exchanges, it uses a daily prompt date system, so you can trade copper for delivery on any business day out to 3 months. The cash to 3 month spread tells you how tight the physical market is: backwardation means buyers are scrambling for nearby metal; contango means metal is sitting in warehouses with no urgency. Most physical contracts globally price off LME plus a regional premium.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Contract size:** 25 metric tonnes per lot for all 6 base metals.

**Prompt date structure:**
- Cash (spot): delivery in 2 business days
- Daily dates: out to 3 months (every business day)
- Weekly dates: 3 to 6 months
- Monthly dates: 7 to 123 months depending on metal

**Trading venues:**
- Ring (open outcry): 11:40 AM to 5:00 PM London, sets official settlement prices
- LMEselect (electronic): 01:00 to 19:00 London
- Inter-office (OTC): 24 hours

**Settlement:** Physical delivery to or from LME approved warehouses globally.

## Common misconceptions

**"The LME is just another futures exchange."** The prompt date system, physical warehouse network, and ring trading make it fundamentally different from CME or ICE. The differences matter.

**"LME warehouse stocks represent total global inventory."** LME stocks are only the visible, deliverable portion. Off warrant stocks in non LME warehouses can be multiples of on warrant stocks.

**"The LME nickel squeeze of 2022 was a one off."** Squeezes have occurred multiple times across LME metals. The physical delivery mechanism makes it inherently susceptible to delivery squeezes.
