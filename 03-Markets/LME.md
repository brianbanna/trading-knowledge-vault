# LME

## Definition

The London Metal Exchange is the world's premier exchange for base metals trading, handling over $14 trillion in annual notional volume. Founded in 1877, the LME trades six primary contracts: [[Copper Futures|copper]], [[Aluminum Futures|aluminum]], [[Zinc Futures|zinc]], [[Nickel Futures|nickel]], [[Lead Futures|lead]], and [[Tin Futures|tin]]. What makes the LME unique is its prompt date system: instead of monthly expiry contracts like COMEX or SHFE, the LME trades on specific calendar dates (daily out to 3 months, then weekly, then monthly). The 3 month contract is the most liquid. The LME also operates a global network of approved warehouses where physical metal can be delivered, stored, and withdrawn.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

LME prices are the global reference for industrial metals. Virtually every physical metal contract in the world is priced as a premium or discount to the LME benchmark. The LME [[Cash to 3 Month Spread]] is the key indicator of physical market tightness. [[LME Warehouse Stocks]] and [[Cancelled Warrants]] provide real time inventory signals. For FX traders, LME metals prices drive [[Commodity Currencies]] like AUD, CLP, and ZMW. The LME's unique daily prompt date structure also creates more granular spread trading opportunities than standard monthly futures.

## Concrete example

A metals trader observes that LME copper cash is at $9,850/tonne while the 3 month contract is at $9,780, a $70 backwardation. [[Cancelled Warrants]] are rising (physical demand). She goes long the cash/3 month spread expecting physical tightness to widen the backwardation. If a major smelter in Chile goes offline and copper becomes scarcer, the backwardation could widen to $150, earning $80/tonne. If instead Chinese demand disappoints and metal flows into LME warehouses, the spread could flip to contango at minus $30, losing $100/tonne.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Contract size:** 25 metric tonnes per lot for all six base metals.

**Prompt date structure:**
- Cash (spot): delivery in 2 business days
- Daily dates: out to 3 months (every business day)
- Weekly dates: 3 to 6 months
- Monthly dates: 7 to 123 months (depending on metal)

**Trading venues:**
- Ring (open outcry): 11:40 AM to 5:00 PM London, sets official settlement prices
- LMEselect (electronic): 01:00 to 19:00 London
- Inter-office (OTC): 24 hours

**Settlement:** Physical delivery to/from LME approved warehouses globally.

## Common misconceptions

**"The LME is just another futures exchange."** The prompt date system, physical warehouse network, and ring trading make it fundamentally different from CME or ICE. Understanding these differences is essential.

**"LME warehouse stocks represent total global inventory."** LME stocks are only the visible, deliverable portion. Off warrant stocks (metal in non LME warehouses) can be multiples of on warrant stocks.

**"The LME nickel squeeze of 2022 was a one off event."** Squeezes have occurred multiple times on the LME across different metals. The exchange's physical delivery mechanism makes it inherently susceptible to delivery squeezes.
