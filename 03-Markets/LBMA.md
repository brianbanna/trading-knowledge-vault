# LBMA

## Definition

The London Bullion Market Association is the international trade association for the global OTC gold and silver market. LBMA oversees the largest physical precious metals market in the world, where bullion is traded between member banks, refiners, and dealers. The market is principal to principal (OTC, not exchange traded), with settlement through London Precious Metals Clearing Limited (LPMCL). LBMA administers the twice daily gold price benchmark (LBMA Gold Price, formerly the London Fix) and the daily silver price benchmark. London Good Delivery bars (gold: 350 to 430 troy oz, ~400 oz standard; silver: 750 to 1,100 troy oz) are the international bullion standard.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

LBMA gold and silver prices are the global reference benchmarks used for derivative settlement, ETF creation/redemption, central bank transactions, and mine production pricing. The [[London Fix]] is referenced by virtually every gold linked financial product globally. LBMA member banks (HSBC, JP Morgan, UBS, ICBC Standard) provide the liquidity that makes the gold market function. Understanding LBMA matters because the OTC market handles larger flows than [[COMEX]] futures. LBMA clearing data (published weekly) provides insight into wholesale activity and positioning.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** A central bank wants to buy 10 tonnes of gold for reserves. It does not trade COMEX (too large, would move the market). It places the order through an LBMA dealer bank. The bank sources Good Delivery bars from its vaulting network (London, Zurich, New York) and settles via LPMCL. Transaction references the LBMA Gold Price for settlement. A trader monitoring LBMA clearing volumes notices a spike in allocated gold transfers, infers official sector buying, and goes long 30 COMEX gold contracts at $2,400/oz. Gold rallies to $2,460 over 2 weeks. Gain: $60 × 100 oz × 30 = $180,000.

**Simplified:** When central banks, miners, refiners, or large institutions need to move physical gold, they do it through London, not through futures exchanges. LBMA is the trade body that organizes that market and sets the daily benchmark price the rest of the world references. Watching LBMA clearing data is one of the few ways outsiders can see wholesale physical flow before it shows up in retail or futures pricing.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**London Good Delivery standards:**
- Gold bars: 350 to 430 troy oz (~400 oz standard), minimum 995 fineness
- Silver bars: 750 to 1,100 troy oz, minimum 999 fineness

**LBMA Gold Price:** Electronic auction twice daily at 10:30 AM and 3:00 PM London. Participants submit buy/sell orders at proposed prices until supply and demand balance.

**LBMA member types:**
- Market makers: provide two way quotes (banks, trading houses)
- Full members: trade on the market
- Associates: refiners, miners, fabricators

## Common misconceptions

**"COMEX is the gold market."** COMEX is the largest futures market, but LBMA OTC handles more physical flow and is where wholesale participants trade.

**"The LBMA fix can be easily manipulated."** The benchmark was reformed in 2015 (gold) and 2014 (silver) to use electronic auctions with regulatory oversight, replacing the old telephone fix.

**"LBMA is only relevant for physical gold."** LBMA forward rates, lease rates, and clearing data are important signals for all gold market participants, including futures and options traders.
