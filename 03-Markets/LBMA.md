# LBMA

## Definition

The London Bullion Market Association is the international trade association representing the global over the counter (OTC) market for gold and silver. The LBMA oversees the largest physical precious metals market in the world, where gold and silver are traded between member banks, refiners, and dealers. The market operates on a principal to principal basis (OTC, not exchange traded), with settlement through the London Precious Metals Clearing Limited (LPMCL). The LBMA administers the twice daily gold price benchmark (LBMA Gold Price, formerly the London Fix) and the daily silver price benchmark. London Good Delivery bars (gold: 350 to 430 troy ounces, approximately 400 oz standard; silver: 750 to 1,100 troy ounces) are the standard for international bullion trading.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

The LBMA gold and silver prices are the global reference benchmarks used for settlement of derivative contracts, ETF creation/redemption, central bank transactions, and mine production pricing. The [[London Fix]] is referenced by virtually every gold linked financial product globally. LBMA member banks (HSBC, JP Morgan, UBS, ICBC Standard) provide the liquidity that makes the gold market function. For traders, understanding the LBMA structure matters because the OTC market handles larger flows than [[COMEX]] futures, and LBMA clearing data (published weekly) provides insight into wholesale market activity and positioning.

## Concrete example

A central bank wants to buy 10 tonnes of gold for its reserves. It does not trade on COMEX (too large, would move the market) but instead places the order through an LBMA dealer bank. The bank sources Good Delivery bars from its vaulting network (London, Zurich, New York) and settles via LPMCL. The transaction references the LBMA Gold Price for settlement. A trader monitoring LBMA clearing volumes notices a spike in allocated gold transfers, suggesting official sector buying, and goes long gold futures.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**London Good Delivery standards:**
- Gold bars: 350 to 430 troy ounces (standard ~400 oz), minimum 995 fineness
- Silver bars: 750 to 1,100 troy ounces, minimum 999 fineness

**LBMA Gold Price:** Electronic auction held twice daily at 10:30 AM and 3:00 PM London time. Participants submit buy/sell orders at proposed prices until supply and demand balance.

**LBMA member types:**
- Market makers: provide two way quotes (banks, trading houses)
- Full members: trade on the market
- Associates: refiners, miners, fabricators

## Common misconceptions

**"COMEX is the gold market."** COMEX is the largest futures market, but the LBMA OTC market handles more physical gold flow and is where wholesale market participants trade.

**"The LBMA fix can be easily manipulated."** The benchmark was reformed in 2015 (gold) and 2014 (silver) to use electronic auctions with regulatory oversight, replacing the old telephone based fix.

**"LBMA is only relevant for physical gold."** LBMA forward rates, lease rates, and clearing data are important signals for all gold market participants, including futures and options traders.
