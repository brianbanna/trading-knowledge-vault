# OPEC

## Definition

OPEC (Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries) is a cartel of 13 oil producing nations that coordinate production levels to influence global crude prices. Founded in 1960 in Baghdad. Current members: Saudi Arabia (de facto leader and largest producer), Iraq, Iran, UAE, Kuwait, Libya, Nigeria, Algeria, Angola, Congo, Gabon, Equatorial Guinea, Venezuela. Primary tool: setting production quotas for each member, managing ~30% of global oil supply. Saudi Arabia holds the most [[Spare Capacity]] and acts as swing producer, able to move output by several million barrels per day.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

OPEC decisions are the single biggest scheduled catalyst for oil prices. Quota announcements at ministerial meetings (June and November/December in Vienna) move crude $3 to $10 in a single session. Beyond scheduled meetings, unscheduled communications, emergency meetings, and bilateral negotiations between Saudi Arabia and other members generate constant headlines. Understanding internal politics matters: Saudi Arabia wants price stability and market share, UAE wants higher quotas to monetize low cost reserves, smaller members often overproduce. Compliance monitoring is itself a tradeable signal.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** November 2022. OPEC+ announced a surprise 2 million barrel per day production cut. Brent rallied from $92 to $98 in the session. A trader long ahead of the meeting, anticipating Saudi Arabia's desire to defend $90, earned $6/bbl. A trader short (expecting OPEC to maintain output to keep prices moderate for consuming nations) lost $6/bbl and faced margin calls. OPEC meetings are binary events where being on the wrong side carries significant risk.

**Simplified:** A group of oil producing countries coordinates how much oil they pump. Pump less, prices rise. Pump more, prices fall. They meet twice a year to set quotas. Markets move sharply when actual decisions surprise expectations. Saudi Arabia leads, with the largest spare capacity to back up commitments.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**OPEC's market share:** ~30% of global oil production (~30 million bpd out of ~100 million global).

**Compliance rate:** `Compliance = Actual Production Cut / Announced Cut × 100%`

Compliance below 80% signals cheating on quotas. Above 100% means overachieving.

**Spare capacity indicator:** Saudi Arabia holds 1.5 to 3 million bpd of spare capacity. Below 1.5 mb/d, the market loses its buffer and [[Geopolitical Risk]] premium surges.

## Common misconceptions

**"OPEC controls the oil price."** OPEC influences but does not control prices. US shale, demand destruction, and non OPEC supply growth limit OPEC's pricing power. OPEC manages supply within a range, cannot set an exact price.

**"All OPEC members cooperate."** Internal tensions are constant. UAE has pushed for higher quotas, Iran and Venezuela face sanctions limiting output, smaller members routinely exceed quotas when enforcement is lax.

**"OPEC announcements always move the market in the expected direction."** Markets price expectations before announcements. A surprise cut that was leaked or anticipated may cause a sell the news reaction if the actual cut is smaller than expected.
