# OPEC

## Definition

OPEC (Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries) is a cartel of 13 oil producing nations that collectively coordinate production levels to influence global crude oil prices. Founded in 1960 in Baghdad, its current members include Saudi Arabia (the de facto leader and largest producer), Iraq, Iran, UAE, Kuwait, Libya, Nigeria, Algeria, Angola, Congo, Gabon, Equatorial Guinea, and Venezuela. OPEC's primary tool is setting production quotas for each member, effectively managing roughly 30% of global oil supply. Saudi Arabia holds the most [[Spare Capacity]] and acts as the swing producer, able to increase or decrease output by several million barrels per day.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

OPEC decisions are the single biggest scheduled catalyst for oil prices. Quota announcements at OPEC ministerial meetings (typically held in June and November/December in Vienna) can move crude $3 to $10 in a single session. Beyond scheduled meetings, unscheduled communications, emergency meetings, and bilateral negotiations between Saudi Arabia and other members generate constant headlines. For traders, understanding OPEC dynamics means understanding the internal politics: Saudi Arabia wants price stability and market share, UAE wants higher quotas to monetize its low cost reserves, and smaller members often overproduce relative to quotas. Compliance monitoring is itself a tradeable signal.

## Concrete example

In November 2022, OPEC+ announced a surprise 2 million barrel per day production cut. Brent crude rallied from $92 to $98 in the session following the announcement. A trader who had positioned long ahead of the meeting, anticipating Saudi Arabia's desire to defend $90, earned $6/bbl. However, a trader who was short (expecting OPEC to maintain output to keep prices moderate for consuming nations) lost $6/bbl and faced margin calls. The lesson: OPEC meetings are binary events where being on the wrong side carries significant risk.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**OPEC's market share:** ~30% of global oil production (~30 million barrels per day out of ~100 million global).

**Compliance rate:** `Compliance = Actual Production Cut / Announced Cut × 100%`

Compliance below 80% signals that members are cheating on quotas. Above 100% means overachieving.

**Spare capacity indicator:** Saudi Arabia typically holds 1.5 to 3 million barrels per day of spare capacity. When this falls below 1.5 mb/d, the market loses its buffer and [[Geopolitical Risk]] premium surges.

## Common misconceptions

**"OPEC controls the oil price."** OPEC influences but does not control prices. US shale, demand destruction, and non OPEC supply growth all limit OPEC's pricing power. OPEC manages supply within a range but cannot set an exact price.

**"All OPEC members cooperate."** Internal tensions are constant. UAE has pushed for higher quotas, Iran and Venezuela face sanctions limiting their output, and smaller members routinely exceed quotas when enforcement is lax.

**"OPEC announcements always move the market in the expected direction."** Markets price expectations before announcements. A "surprise" cut that was leaked or anticipated may cause a "sell the news" reaction if the actual cut is smaller than expected.
