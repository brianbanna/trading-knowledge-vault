# OPEC+

## Definition

OPEC+ is the broader coalition of oil producing countries: the 13 [[OPEC]] members plus 10 non OPEC partners, most notably Russia, Kazakhstan, Mexico, Oman, Azerbaijan. Formed in 2016 when oil collapsed below $30/bbl to coordinate production cuts beyond OPEC's traditional membership. OPEC+ controls ~50% of global oil production and the vast majority of the world's [[Spare Capacity]]. Operates through a Joint Ministerial Monitoring Committee (JMMC) that meets regularly to assess market conditions and recommend production adjustments.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

OPEC+ decisions drive oil markets more than any other single factor. Adding Russia, the world's 2nd or 3rd largest producer, gave the coalition far greater market control than OPEC alone but introduced more complex internal politics. Saudi Arabia and Russia don't always agree on strategy: Russia historically favors market share over price support, while Saudi Arabia prioritizes price stability. The UAE has pushed for higher baseline quotas. Internal tensions create uncertainty, and the market watches for signs of fracture. OPEC+ compliance, overproduction by individual members, and the pace of unwinding cuts are tradeable signals.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Early 2020. OPEC+ negotiations collapsed when Russia refused Saudi Arabia's proposed cut in response to COVID demand destruction. Saudi Arabia retaliated by flooding the market, increasing output to over 12 mb/d. Brent crashed from $50 to $20 in 3 weeks. Traders who recognized the OPEC+ breakdown as a supply shock and positioned short earned $30/bbl. The subsequent record OPEC+ cut of 9.7 mb/d in April 2020 drove prices back up. This episode illustrates both the power of the coalition and the catastrophic consequences when it fractures.

**Simplified:** OPEC plus Russia and a few other big producers. Together they control about half of world oil. They coordinate cuts and increases to manage prices. When they agree, oil prices follow their decisions. When they disagree publicly (like Saudi Arabia vs Russia in 2020), prices crash because the coalition's discipline breaks.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**OPEC+ production capacity:** ~50 million bpd out of ~100 million global.

**Baseline quotas:** Each member has an agreed baseline production level. Cuts are expressed as reductions from this baseline.

**Compliance monitoring:** Monthly production figures tracked by secondary sources (IEA, OPEC Monthly Report, Argus, Reuters surveys).

**Key internal dynamics to watch:**
- Saudi/Russia relationship (the "Riyadh-Moscow axis")
- UAE quota dissatisfaction
- African member underproduction (often involuntary due to investment shortfalls)

## Common misconceptions

**"OPEC+ always sticks to its announced cuts."** Compliance is never 100%. Russia in particular has a history of slow implementation. Some African members overproduce, others underproduce involuntarily.

**"OPEC+ acts as a unified block."** Internal tensions are constant and drive the largest market moves. The 2020 price war started precisely because the coalition fractured.

**"Unwinding cuts is straightforward."** OPEC+ has repeatedly delayed planned production increases because market conditions deteriorated. The pace of unwinding is itself a negotiated and market moving decision.
