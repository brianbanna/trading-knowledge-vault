# OPEC+

## Definition

OPEC+ is the broader coalition of oil producing countries that includes the 13 [[OPEC]] members plus 10 non OPEC partners, most notably Russia, Kazakhstan, Mexico, Oman, Azerbaijan, and others. Formed in 2016 when oil prices collapsed below $30/bbl, the alliance was created to coordinate production cuts beyond OPEC's traditional membership. Together, OPEC+ controls approximately 50% of global oil production and holds the vast majority of the world's [[Spare Capacity]]. The alliance operates through a Joint Ministerial Monitoring Committee (JMMC) that meets regularly to assess market conditions and recommend production adjustments.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

OPEC+ decisions drive oil markets more than any other single factor. The addition of Russia, the world's 2nd or 3rd largest producer, gave the coalition far greater market control than OPEC alone. However, this also introduced more complex internal politics. Saudi Arabia and Russia do not always agree on strategy: Russia has historically favored market share over price support, while Saudi Arabia prioritizes price stability. The UAE has pushed for higher baseline quotas. These internal tensions create uncertainty, and the market closely watches for signs of fracture. OPEC+ compliance, overproduction by individual members, and the pace of unwinding cuts are all tradeable signals.

## Concrete example

In early 2020, OPEC+ negotiations collapsed when Russia refused Saudi Arabia's proposed production cut in response to COVID demand destruction. Saudi Arabia retaliated by flooding the market, increasing output to over 12 mb/d. Brent crashed from $50 to $20 in 3 weeks. Traders who recognized the OPEC+ breakdown as a supply shock event and positioned short earned $30/bbl. The subsequent record OPEC+ cut of 9.7 mb/d in April 2020 then drove prices back up. This episode illustrates both the power of the coalition and the catastrophic consequences when it fractures.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**OPEC+ production capacity:** ~50 million barrels per day out of ~100 million global.

**Baseline quotas:** Each member has an agreed baseline production level. Cuts are expressed as reductions from this baseline.

**Compliance monitoring:** Monthly production figures tracked by secondary sources (IEA, OPEC Monthly Report, Argus, Reuters surveys).

**Key internal dynamics to watch:**
- Saudi/Russia relationship (the "Riyadh-Moscow axis")
- UAE quota dissatisfaction
- African member underproduction (often involuntary due to investment shortfalls)

## Common misconceptions

**"OPEC+ always sticks to its announced cuts."** Compliance is never 100%. Russia in particular has a history of slow implementation. Some African members overproduce, while others underproduce involuntarily.

**"OPEC+ acts as a unified block."** Internal tensions are constant and often drive the largest market moves. The 2020 price war started precisely because the coalition fractured.

**"Unwinding cuts is straightforward."** OPEC+ has repeatedly delayed planned production increases because market conditions deteriorated. The pace of unwinding is itself a negotiated and market moving decision.
