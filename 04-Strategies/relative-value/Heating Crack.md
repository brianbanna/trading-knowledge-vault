# Heating Crack

## Definition

The heating crack spread is the refining margin for middle distillates: heating oil/gasoil price minus crude price. In the US: ULSD (Ultra Low Sulfur Diesel, NYMEX HO) minus [[WTI Crude Oil]]. In Europe: ICE Gasoil minus [[Brent Crude]]. The diesel/heating oil equivalent of the [[Gasoline Crack]] and one of the 2 legs of the 3:2:1 [[Crack Spread]]. Strongly seasonal: widens in Q4/Q1 when Northern Hemisphere heating demand peaks, narrows in summer.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

The heating crack is one of the most important margin indicators in energy. Diesel powers global commerce (trucking, shipping, rail, agriculture), so the heating crack reflects real economic activity more directly than the gasoline crack. European gasoil cracks are closely watched because Europe is structurally short distillate (imports from Russia, Middle East, US). Loss of Russian diesel supply post 2022 sanctions structurally widened European gasoil cracks. Tradeable on its own, as a seasonal spread, or as the relative value leg of a gasoline vs heating oil spread.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** October 2025. Trader goes long the European gasoil crack (long ICE Gasoil, short Brent) at $22/bbl, expecting cold winter to boost heating demand. By January 2026, severe cold snap across Northern Europe drives heating oil demand to multi year highs. Gasoil crack widens to $34/bbl. Profit $12/bbl. Counter case: mild winter, demand disappoints, crack narrows to $17/bbl. Loss $5/bbl.

**Simplified:** Refineries turn crude into diesel and heating oil. The gap between distillate and crude prices is the refiner's margin on those products. Winter heating in Europe and the US Northeast spikes demand. Buy distillate futures, sell crude, hold from autumn to mid winter. The trade is the seasonal margin expansion in middle distillates.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Heating crack:** `Heating Crack = Heating Oil Price ($/bbl) - Crude Oil Price ($/bbl)`

**Unit conversion:** NYMEX heating oil is in cents per gallon. `$/bbl = $/gal × 42`

**Seasonal pattern:**
- Widens: October to February (winter heating demand)
- Narrows: April to August (lower heating demand)

**European gasoil crack:** `Gasoil Crack = ICE Gasoil ($/mt) / 7.45 - Brent ($/bbl)` (1 metric tonne gasoil ≈ 7.45 barrels)

## Common misconceptions

**"The heating crack only matters in winter."** Diesel is used year round for transport, industrial activity, and agriculture. Winter heating adds incremental demand on top of a significant base.

**"US and European heating cracks move together."** Correlated but diverge significantly due to different crude quality slates, refinery configurations, and trade flow dynamics. European gasoil cracks have their own drivers.

**"The heating crack is simple to trade."** 2 legs with different contract sizes, quotation conventions (cents/gallon vs $/barrel), and expiry dates. Unit conversion errors are a common trap.
