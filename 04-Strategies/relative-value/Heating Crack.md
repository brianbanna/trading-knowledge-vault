# Heating Crack

## Definition

The heating crack spread is the refining margin for middle distillates, calculated as the price of heating oil (ULSD/gasoil) minus the price of crude oil. In the US, it is expressed as ULSD (Ultra Low Sulfur Diesel, NYMEX HO contract) minus [[WTI Crude Oil]]. In Europe, it is ICE Gasoil minus [[Brent Crude]]. The heating crack is the diesel/heating oil equivalent of the [[Gasoline Crack]] and is one of the two legs of the standard 3:2:1 [[Crack Spread]] (2 barrels gasoline + 1 barrel diesel from 3 barrels crude). It is strongly seasonal, widening in Q4/Q1 when heating demand peaks in the Northern Hemisphere and narrowing in summer when distillate demand is less intense.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

The heating crack is one of the most important margin indicators in energy. Diesel is the fuel of global commerce (trucking, shipping, rail, agriculture), so the heating crack reflects real economic activity more directly than the gasoline crack. European gasoil cracks are particularly closely watched because Europe is structurally short distillate (imports from Russia, the Middle East, and the US). The loss of Russian diesel supply post 2022 sanctions structurally widened European gasoil cracks. For traders, the heating crack is tradeable on its own, as a seasonal spread, or as the relative value leg of a gasoline vs heating oil spread.

## Concrete example

In October 2025, a trader goes long the European gasoil crack (long ICE Gasoil, short Brent) at $22/bbl, expecting a cold winter to boost heating demand. By January 2026, a severe cold snap across Northern Europe drives heating oil demand to multi year highs. The gasoil crack widens to $34/bbl, and the trader profits $12/bbl. If the winter had been mild, demand would have disappointed, and the crack could have narrowed to $17/bbl, losing $5/bbl.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Heating crack:** `Heating Crack = Heating Oil Price ($/bbl) - Crude Oil Price ($/bbl)`

**Unit conversion:** NYMEX heating oil is quoted in cents per gallon. To convert: `$/bbl = $/gal × 42`

**Seasonal pattern:**
- Widens: October to February (winter heating demand)
- Narrows: April to August (lower heating demand)

**European gasoil crack:** `Gasoil Crack = ICE Gasoil ($/mt) / 7.45 - Brent ($/bbl)` (approximate conversion: 1 metric tonne gasoil ≈ 7.45 barrels)

## Common misconceptions

**"The heating crack only matters in winter."** Diesel is used year round for transport, industrial activity, and agriculture. Winter heating adds incremental demand, but the base level is always significant.

**"US and European heating cracks move together."** They are correlated but can diverge significantly due to different crude quality slates, refinery configurations, and trade flow dynamics. European gasoil cracks have their own drivers.

**"The heating crack is simple to trade."** The crack involves two legs (product and crude) with different contract sizes, quotation conventions (cents/gallon vs $/barrel), and expiry dates. Unit conversion errors are a common trap.
