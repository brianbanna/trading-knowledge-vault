# Crack Spread

## Core Thesis

The crack spread replicates refinery economics: buy crude, sell refined products (gasoline and heating oil/diesel). It captures refining margins. Not a directional bet on oil but a bet on the profitability of turning crude into products.

## Mechanics

### The Standard 3:2:1 Crack
Buy 3 crude contracts, sell 2 gasoline contracts, sell 1 heating oil (ULSD) contract. Approximates typical refinery output ratio.

### Entry Criteria
- Margins deviating from seasonal norms
- Turnaround season (planned refinery maintenance) reducing product supply
- Demand shock in products (hurricane season driving gasoline demand)

### Exit Criteria
- Margins revert to seasonal average
- Time based: exit before seasonal pattern reverses
