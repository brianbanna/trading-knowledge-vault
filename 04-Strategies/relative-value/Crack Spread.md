# Crack Spread

## Core Thesis

The crack spread replicates the economics of an oil refinery by buying crude oil and selling refined products (gasoline and heating oil/diesel). The spread captures refining margins. It is not a directional bet on oil prices but a bet on the profitability of turning crude into products.

## Mechanics

### The Standard 3:2:1 Crack
Buy 3 crude oil contracts, sell 2 gasoline contracts, sell 1 heating oil (ULSD) contract. This approximates the typical refinery output ratio.

### Entry Criteria
- Refining margins deviating from seasonal norms
- Turnaround season (planned refinery maintenance) reducing supply of products
- Demand shock in products (e.g., hurricane season driving gasoline demand)

### Exit Criteria
- Margins revert to seasonal average
- Time based: exit before seasonal pattern reverses
