# Gold-Copper Ratio

## Core Thesis

The Gold-Copper ratio divides gold by copper to capture the market's view on global growth vs fear. Gold is the safe haven, rising when real rates fall and central banks ease. Copper is "Dr Copper," rising when construction booms, manufacturing expands, and China grows. Rising ratio = fear winning (gold up, copper down). Falling ratio = optimism (copper up, gold flat or lagging). Trading the ratio expresses a macro view while hedging the broad metals direction.

## Mechanics

### Calculating the Ratio

**Gold-Copper Ratio = Gold price ($/oz) / Copper price ($/lb)**

Example: Gold at $2,300/oz, copper at $4.20/lb. Ratio = 548.

Futures: COMEX gold (GC, 100 troy oz) vs COMEX copper (HG, 25,000 lbs). Ratio commonly uses spot prices in $/oz and $/lb.

Historical range: ~300 to 800 over 20 years. Average 450 to 550. Spiked to 750+ during COVID (March 2020). Dropped to 350 during China infrastructure boom (2006, 2011).

### Entry Criteria
- Ratio deviates from 2 year mean by more than 1.5 [[Z-Score|standard deviations]]
- Macro catalyst aligned with the trade: ratio >700 needs growth recovery thesis (China stimulus, rate cuts). Ratio <350 needs risk off thesis.
- Confirmation from ISM Manufacturing, China PMI, [[TIPS Breakeven|breakevens]], credit spreads

### Exit Criteria
- Ratio reverts to mean or overshoots
- Macro thesis invalidated (expected recovery but PMI keeps falling)
- Time stop: macro mean reversion takes months. 8 to 12 week minimum holding period.

### Position Sizing
Dollar neutral: match notional. GC = 100 oz x $2,300 = $230,000. HG = 25,000 lbs x $4.20 = $105,000. ~1 GC : 2 HG for dollar neutral. COMEX margin credit available for recognized inter commodity spreads.

## Instruments Used
- [[Gold Futures]] COMEX (GC)
- [[Copper Futures]] COMEX (HG) or LME copper
- ETFs as proxy: GLD vs COPX or similar (less precise, no leverage)

## Risk Profile

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Primary Risk | Macro regime persists longer than expected (growth stays weak or strong for years) |
| Max Loss Scenario | [[Structural Break]]: gold reprices permanently higher on central bank buying while copper demand structurally declines, or vice versa |
| Hedged Exposure | General metals direction partially cancelled |
| Unhedged Exposure | Macro regime risk, China demand shocks, central bank gold policy, supply disruptions |
| Margin Requirements | COMEX inter commodity spread credit available |

## Edge Source

Macro expression trade, not fundamental commodity trade. Edge comes from reading the global growth cycle, China's credit impulse (copper demand), and central bank policy (gold). The ratio correlates strongly with ISM Manufacturing PMI, US 10 year yield, and China's credit impulse. Traders who time cyclical turns have an edge. Physical anchoring is weaker than processing spreads (gold and copper have no substitution or processing link), so mean reversion is slower and driven by macro cycles.

## When It Works / When It Fails

### Works Well When
- Clear macro inflection points: recession ending (short ratio = long copper, short gold), expansion ending (long ratio = long gold, short copper)
- China stimulus announcements (compress the ratio rapidly as copper surges)
- Fed easing cycles beginning (depends on which metal benefits more)

### Fails When
- Gold driven by non macro factors: central bank buying creating structural bid independent of risk sentiment (2022 to 2025 central bank gold accumulation)
- Copper supply disruptions move copper independently of demand (mine strikes, export bans)
- Ratio trends for extended periods during sustained macro regimes (2001 to 2006 China boom kept the ratio compressed for 5 years)
- Correlation between gold and copper rises during broad commodity liquidation

## Historical Examples

**March 2020 COVID Crash:** Ratio spiked from 500 to 750+ as gold held firm while copper collapsed to $2.10/lb. Traders who shorted the ratio at the extreme and held through recovery were rewarded as Chinese stimulus pulled copper to $4.70/lb by 2021 while gold drifted, compressing the ratio back to 400.

**2011 Copper Peak:** Copper hit $4.60/lb on Chinese infrastructure demand. Gold at $1,500/oz. Ratio = 326. Extreme low. Over 4 years copper halved while gold declined less, pushing the ratio back above 600.

**2022 to 2025 Divergence:** Central bank gold buying (China, India, Turkey, Poland) pushed gold above $2,000 while copper struggled on weak Chinese property. Ratio stayed elevated, frustrating mean reversion traders. Structural central bank demand broke the historical relationship.

## Concrete Example (Current or Recent)

**Concrete:** Gold at $2,300/oz. Copper at $4.10/lb. Ratio = 561. 2 year average 530, sigma 70. Current 0.4 sigma above mean, not extreme. Watching China's March PMI. If PMI prints >52 (expansion) and ratio is still above 560, short the ratio: sell 1 GC at $2,300 (notional $230,000), buy 2 HG at $4.10 (notional $205,000). Target: ratio compresses to 480 (a 14% move). If gold stays at $2,300, copper needs to reach $4.79/lb. P&L on 2 HG: ($4.79 - $4.10) x 25,000 x 2 = $34,500 gain. Gold flat. Total ~$34,500 profit. Risk: ratio expands to 620. Stop loss at 600.

**Simplified:** Gold goes up when people are scared. Copper goes up when factories are humming. Divide gold by copper to get a single fear-vs-growth gauge. Trade the ratio when it gets extreme: when fear has won too much (high ratio), bet on growth coming back. When growth has run too far (low ratio), bet on fear returning. Slow trade, takes months.
