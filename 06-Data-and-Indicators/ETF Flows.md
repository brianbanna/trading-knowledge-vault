# ETF Flows

## Definition

ETF flows in precious metals are the physical metal bought or sold by ETFs in response to investor inflows and outflows. The largest gold ETF, SPDR Gold Shares (GLD), holds over 800 tonnes in London vaults. Investor buying triggers authorized participants to deposit physical gold, expanding holdings. Selling triggers redemption for physical, reducing holdings. The daily change in holdings is published and serves as a real time investment demand indicator. Silver (SLV, SIVR) and platinum (PPLT) ETFs follow the same mechanism.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

ETF flows are the most visible investment demand indicator for precious metals. Persistent inflows are bullish; outflows bearish. In 2020, gold ETFs added 870+ tonnes (record), contributing to gold's rally to $2,075. In 2022 to 2023, ETFs shed over 800 tonnes as rising real rates reduced gold's appeal. For traders, ETF flows distinguish rallies supported by real investment demand (sustainable) from rallies driven by speculative futures positioning (less sustainable). Daily data is available from the World Gold Council and fund sponsors.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Gold rallies $200 from $2,200 to $2,400 over 2 months. Trader checks GLD: holdings up just 5 tonnes (minimal inflow). [[COT Report]] shows MM adding 80,000 contracts. Rally is speculative futures, not physical. Trader shorts 30 COMEX gold contracts at $2,400. Specs liquidate over 3 weeks, gold drops to $2,300. Gain: $100/oz × 100 oz × 30 = $300,000. Counter scenario: belated physical ETF buying validates the move and gold pushes to $2,500. Same short loses $300,000.

**Simplified:** When investors buy gold ETFs, the fund must buy physical gold to back the shares. The daily change in ETF holdings tells you how much real money is being put into gold. Speculative futures rallies look identical on a price chart but lack ETF inflows. If futures positioning is bullish but ETF flows are flat or negative, the rally is fragile and likely to fade.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Creation/redemption:**
- Inflow: investors buy shares → AP deposits physical metal → holdings rise
- Outflow: investors sell shares → AP redeems for physical → holdings fall

**Major precious metals ETFs:**
| ETF | Metal | Holdings (approx) |
|---|---|---|
| GLD (SPDR Gold Shares) | Gold | ~870 tonnes |
| IAU (iShares Gold Trust) | Gold | ~450 tonnes |
| SLV (iShares Silver Trust) | Silver | ~13,000 tonnes |
| PPLT (Aberdeen Platinum) | Platinum | ~15 tonnes |

## Common misconceptions

**"ETF buying creates new gold demand."** ETFs redistribute existing above ground gold from vaults to the ETF trust. Ownership changes, not total supply.

**"ETF holdings always move with the gold price."** Prices can rally on futures speculation while ETFs see outflows. This happened in 2024 when central bank buying drove gold higher despite ETF selling.

**"ETF flows are the most important demand indicator."** For gold, central bank buying now exceeds ETF flows in annual volume. Track both.
