# ETF Flows

## Definition

ETF flows in precious metals refer to the buying and selling of physical metal by exchange traded funds in response to investor inflows and outflows. The largest gold ETF, SPDR Gold Shares (GLD), holds over 800 tonnes of gold in London vaults. When investors buy GLD shares, authorized participants create new shares by depositing physical gold, increasing the fund's metal holdings. When investors sell, shares are redeemed for physical gold, reducing holdings. The daily change in ETF holdings is published and serves as a transparent, real time indicator of investment demand. Silver ETFs (SLV, SIVR) and platinum ETFs (PPLT) follow the same mechanism.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

ETF flows are one of the most visible and immediate demand/supply indicators for precious metals. Persistent inflows signal sustained investment demand and are bullish. Large outflows signal liquidation and are bearish. In 2020, gold ETFs added over 870 tonnes (record annual inflow), contributing to gold's rally to $2,075. In 2022 to 2023, ETFs shed over 800 tonnes as rising real rates reduced gold's attractiveness. For traders, tracking the pace and direction of ETF flows provides insight into whether price moves are supported by real investment demand (sustainable) or driven purely by speculative futures positioning (less sustainable). ETF flow data is available daily from the World Gold Council and individual fund sponsors.

## Concrete example

Gold rallies from $2,200 to $2,400 over 2 months. A trader checks GLD holdings: they have increased by only 5 tonnes (minimal inflow). Meanwhile, [[COT Report]] shows managed money adding 80,000 contracts. The rally is driven by speculative futures buying, not ETF investment. The trader goes short, expecting a positioning correction. If specs liquidate, gold drops to $2,300, earning $100/oz. If physical ETF buying kicks in belatedly, validating the rally, gold could push to $2,500.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Creation/redemption mechanism:**
- Inflow: investors buy ETF shares → authorized participant deposits physical metal → ETF holdings increase
- Outflow: investors sell ETF shares → authorized participant redeems for physical metal → ETF holdings decrease

**Major precious metals ETFs:**
| ETF | Metal | Holdings (approx) |
|---|---|---|
| GLD (SPDR Gold Shares) | Gold | ~870 tonnes |
| IAU (iShares Gold Trust) | Gold | ~450 tonnes |
| SLV (iShares Silver Trust) | Silver | ~13,000 tonnes |
| PPLT (Aberdeen Platinum) | Platinum | ~15 tonnes |

**Data source:** World Gold Council publishes weekly global gold ETF flow data.

## Common misconceptions

**"ETF buying creates new gold demand."** ETFs redistribute existing above ground gold from vaults to the ETF trust. They change the ownership structure, not the total gold supply.

**"ETF holdings always move with the gold price."** Prices can rally on futures speculation while ETFs see outflows (as happened in 2024 when central bank buying drove gold higher despite ETF selling).

**"ETF flows are the most important demand indicator."** For gold, central bank buying now exceeds ETF flows in annual volume. Both must be tracked together.
