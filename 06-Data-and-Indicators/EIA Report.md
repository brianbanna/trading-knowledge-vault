# EIA Report

## Definition

The EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report is published every Wednesday at 10:30 AM ET by the US Energy Information Administration. It is the highest frequency, most market moving fundamental release in energy markets. The report covers US crude inventories, gasoline, distillate (diesel and heating oil), refinery utilization, weekly production estimates, imports, and exports. Cushing, Oklahoma stocks (the [[WTI Crude Oil]] delivery point) are reported separately and closely watched because they drive the WTI front month and [[Calendar Spread]] shape.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

EIA moves oil within seconds. Surprise builds are bearish, surprise draws bullish. The deviation from consensus drives the reaction, not the absolute level. Crude, refined products, and energy equities all respond. The report is the most granular view of US supply and demand and the anchor of any oil trader's weekly workflow. The [[API Report]] on Tuesday evening previews it; the EIA is the official source.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Wednesday 10:30 AM ET. Consensus crude draw 2.0 million barrels. EIA prints a 7.0 million draw, a 5.0 million surprise. WTI front month jumps $1.50 within 30 seconds. Gasoline draws 3.0 million vs 1.0 million expected. Trader long 20 WTI contracts going in gains 20 × 1,000 × $1.50 = $30,000.

**Simplified:** Every Wednesday, the US government publishes how much oil sits in storage. If inventories drew down more than analysts expected, the world is consuming more oil than supplying, so prices jump. If inventories built up more than expected, the opposite. The market reacts in seconds. Most active oil traders trade nothing else that morning except positioning into and out of the print.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Release schedule:** Wednesday 10:30 AM ET, delayed to Thursday if Monday is a federal holiday.

**Key data points:**
- Crude inventories (total US and Cushing separately)
- Gasoline inventories
- Distillate inventories
- Refinery utilization (%)
- Crude production (weekly estimate)
- Crude imports and exports
- Days of supply

**Price sensitivity rule of thumb:** 1 million barrel crude surprise moves WTI ~$0.20 to $0.40, varying with context.

## Common misconceptions

**"The EIA tells you the direction of oil prices."** A single weekly print does not set the trend. It creates short term volatility within the broader supply and demand context.

**"A build is always bearish."** Context matters. A small build during turnaround season (builds expected) is bullish if smaller than feared. Surprise vs consensus drives the reaction.

**"EIA data is perfectly accurate."** Weekly data is survey based and revised frequently. Monthly data (lagged) is more accurate. Weekly production estimates can be off by hundreds of thousands of bbl/day.
