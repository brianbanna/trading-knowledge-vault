# Central Bank Buying

## Definition

Central bank buying is the accumulation of gold reserves by sovereign central banks and monetary authorities. Since 2022, global central banks have purchased above 1,000 tonnes per year, reversing two decades of net selling. The largest buyers are China (PBOC), India (RBI), Turkey, Poland, Czech Republic, and Singapore. Motivations: reserve diversification away from USD, geopolitical hedging (especially after the 2022 freeze of Russian reserves), and demand for an asset with no counterparty risk.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Central bank buying is now the largest single source of gold demand, surpassing jewelry, ETFs, and industrial use. At 1,000+ tonnes per year (about 25% of mine production), official sector buying provides a structural demand floor. The flow is price insensitive (central banks buy on dips and at all time highs), fundamentally different from speculative demand. For gold traders, it explains why gold stayed resilient through rising [[Real Interest Rates]] and a strong USD that historically were bearish. For FX traders, gold accumulation is part of broader de dollarization, affecting USD reserve currency dynamics.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** PBOC reports gold reserve additions of 30 tonnes per month for 18 consecutive months in 2023 to 2024. Gold rallies from $1,900 to $2,400 despite rising US real rates and a strong dollar. Traditional models (gold = inverse real rates) predicted a fall. A trader tracking central bank buying as a structural demand shift went long gold and was rewarded. If PBOC paused (as it occasionally does), the market could correct $100 to $200 as the perceived demand floor weakens.

**Simplified:** Central banks decided after 2022 that holding their reserves in US Treasuries was risky. They started buying gold instead, in size, every month. That buying is constant and price blind. It puts a floor under the gold market that did not exist before. Old rules about gold falling when real rates rise broke because the buyer profile changed.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Annual central bank purchases (2022 to 2025):** 1,000 to 1,200 tonnes per year.

**Key buyers and estimated annual purchases:**
- China (PBOC): 200 to 300 tonnes (reported + unreported)
- India (RBI): 50 to 100 tonnes
- Turkey: 50 to 100 tonnes
- Poland: 80 to 130 tonnes
- Other (Czech Republic, Singapore, etc.): 100 to 200 tonnes

**Share of market:** Central bank buying ≈ 25% of annual mine production (~3,600 tonnes).

**Data sources:** World Gold Council quarterly central bank survey, IMF International Financial Statistics (reported reserves).

## Common misconceptions

**"Central bank buying is transparent."** Many central banks (especially China) underreport purchases. Actual buying is higher than official IMF data suggests.

**"Central bank buying stops when gold gets too expensive."** Central banks are price insensitive strategic buyers. They bought at $1,900, $2,100, and $2,400 without hesitation.

**"This is temporary."** The motivation (reserve diversification, geopolitical hedging) is structural, not cyclical. Barring major geopolitical de escalation, the trend persists.
