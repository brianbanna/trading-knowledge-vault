# TIPS Breakeven

## Definition

The TIPS breakeven inflation rate is the difference between the yield on a nominal US Treasury bond and the yield on a Treasury Inflation Protected Security (TIPS) of the same maturity. It is the market's expectation for average annual inflation over that period. If the 10 year Treasury yields 4.5% and the 10 year TIPS yields 2.0%, the breakeven is 2.5%, meaning the market expects CPI to average 2.5% per year over the next decade. If actual inflation exceeds 2.5%, TIPS outperform nominal Treasuries. Below 2.5%, nominal Treasuries outperform.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

TIPS breakevens are a critical signal for gold, commodities, and FX. Rising breakevens (higher inflation expectations) are bullish for [[Gold Futures|gold]] and real assets because they reduce the opportunity cost of holding non yielding assets relative to bonds whose real return is eroding. For commodity traders, breakevens distinguish between nominal interest rate moves (may not affect commodities) and real rate moves (do). A 50bp rise in nominal yields driven entirely by higher breakevens (inflation expectations up, real rates flat) is neutral to bullish for gold. The same 50bp from higher real rates (breakevens flat) is bearish for gold.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** The 10 year TIPS breakeven rises 2.2% to 2.8% over 3 months as oil spikes and wage growth accelerates. Gold rallies $2,300 to $2,450 as inflation fears support real asset demand. A trader long gold when breakevens started rising profits $150/oz. If breakevens had fallen (deflation scare), gold would have dropped, even if nominal rates fell too.

**Simplified:** Take the regular Treasury yield. Subtract the inflation protected Treasury yield. The difference is what the market thinks inflation will average over that period. When that number rises, gold and commodities catch a bid because they are real assets. When it falls, gold loses its inflation hedge value. Two yields, one ratio, big signal.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Breakeven formula:** `Breakeven = Nominal Treasury Yield - TIPS Yield`

**Key maturities:**
- 5 year breakeven: medium term inflation expectations
- 10 year breakeven: benchmark, most watched
- 30 year breakeven: long term structural inflation view

**Real rate:** `Real Rate = TIPS Yield = Nominal Yield - Breakeven`

**Gold relationship:** Gold price inversely correlated with real rates (TIPS yields), not nominal rates. Rising breakevens with flat real rates = gold bullish.

## Common misconceptions

**"Breakevens perfectly predict future inflation."** Breakevens embed a liquidity premium and risk premium that make them an imperfect forecast. Better as a relative signal than a precise prediction.

**"Rising breakevens are always bullish for gold."** Only if real rates do not rise faster. If nominal rates surge (tightening) and breakevens rise modestly, real rates rise and gold suffers.

**"Only US TIPS breakevens matter."** European (Bund/inflation linked) and UK (Gilt/linker) breakevens provide additional information for global inflation expectations.
