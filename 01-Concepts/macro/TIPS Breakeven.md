# TIPS Breakeven

## Definition

The TIPS breakeven inflation rate is the difference between the yield on a nominal US Treasury bond and the yield on a Treasury Inflation Protected Security (TIPS) of the same maturity. It represents the market's expectation for average annual inflation over that period. For example, if the 10 year Treasury yields 4.5% and the 10 year TIPS yields 2.0%, the breakeven is 2.5%, meaning the market expects CPI inflation to average 2.5% per year over the next decade. If actual inflation exceeds 2.5%, TIPS outperform nominal Treasuries. If inflation is below 2.5%, nominal Treasuries outperform.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

TIPS breakevens are a critical signal for gold, commodities, and FX. Rising breakevens (higher inflation expectations) are bullish for [[Gold Futures|gold]] and real assets because they reduce the opportunity cost of holding non yielding assets relative to bonds whose real return is being eroded. For commodity traders, breakevens help distinguish between nominal interest rate moves (which may not affect commodities) and real rate moves (which do). A 50 basis point rise in nominal yields driven entirely by higher breakevens (inflation expectations up, real rates flat) is neutral to bullish for gold. The same 50 basis point rise from higher real rates (breakevens flat) is bearish for gold.

## Concrete example

The 10 year TIPS breakeven rises from 2.2% to 2.8% over 3 months as oil prices spike and wage growth accelerates. Gold rallies from $2,300 to $2,450 as inflation fears support real asset demand. A trader who went long gold when breakevens started rising profits $150/oz. If breakevens had fallen (deflation scare), gold would likely have dropped as well, even if nominal rates fell too.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Breakeven formula:** `Breakeven = Nominal Treasury Yield - TIPS Yield`

**Key maturities:**
- 5 year breakeven: medium term inflation expectations
- 10 year breakeven: the benchmark, most watched
- 30 year breakeven: long term structural inflation view

**Real rate:** `Real Rate = TIPS Yield = Nominal Yield - Breakeven`

**Gold relationship:** Gold price inversely correlated with real rates (TIPS yields), not nominal rates. Rising breakevens with flat real rates = gold bullish.

## Common misconceptions

**"Breakevens perfectly predict future inflation."** Breakevens embed a liquidity premium and risk premium that make them an imperfect inflation forecast. They are better as a relative signal than as a precise prediction.

**"Rising breakevens are always bullish for gold."** Only if real rates do not rise faster. If nominal rates surge (tightening) and breakevens rise modestly, real rates rise and gold suffers.

**"Only US TIPS breakevens matter."** European (Bund/inflation linked) and UK (Gilt/linker) breakevens provide additional information for global inflation expectations.
