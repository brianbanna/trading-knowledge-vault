# Big Figure

## Definition
The big figure (or "handle") is the integer or round number portion of an exchange rate that traders omit when quoting. In EUR/USD at 1.0853, the big figure is 1.08. Interbank dealers assume both parties know the big figure and quote only the last 2 digits and fractional [[Pip|pips]]: "53" or "53 offered at 55." A move from 1.08 to 1.09 is "1 big figure." For USD/JPY at 150.50, the big figure is 150; a move to 151 is 100 pips. Big figures act as psychological levels where option barriers, stop losses, and order clusters concentrate.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)
Big figures act as natural support and resistance because participants anchor on round numbers. Option dealers set barrier and knockout strikes at big figures (1.1000 EUR/USD), creating hedging flows that pull or push price near these levels. Commodity hedgers executing large FX transactions use big figure context to set limit orders and avoid adverse selection near contested handles. In fast markets, miscommunication about the big figure causes costly trade errors.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** EUR/USD at 1.0850, approaching the 1.0900 handle. Short sellers have stops clustered above 1.0900. Option dealers short 1.0900 call options must buy EUR as spot approaches. Price accelerates through 1.0900 as stops trigger, overshoots to 1.0935, pulls back to 1.0910. A buy limit at 1.0905 just above the figure captures the breakout. A trader who chased the rally at 1.0935 gets stopped at 1.0895 for a 40 pip loss.

**Simplified:** Big round numbers in FX rates act like magnets and walls. Traders place stops just past them, option dealers set barriers on them, and the press anchors stories on them. When price hits the level, all the orders piled up nearby trigger at once, creating a fast move. That move often overshoots and then pulls back, punishing whoever chased the breakout and rewarding whoever waited just past the level.

## Key mechanics and formulas
- **Big figure for EUR/USD at 1.0853**: 1.08. The "small figure" is 53.
- **Big figure for USD/JPY at 150.50**: 150. The dealing price is 50.
- **1 big figure** = 100 [[Pip|pips]] for most pairs. EUR/USD 1.0800 to 1.0900 = 1 big figure.
- **1 big figure for JPY pairs** = 100 pips = 1.00. USD/JPY 150.00 to 151.00 = 1 big figure.
- **Psychological levels**: Double zeros (1.1000, 150.00) and half figures (1.0850, 150.50) attract the most order flow.
- **Dealer quoting**: Only the last 2 digits are quoted voice. "The euro is 50/53" means 1.0850/1.0853.

## Common misconceptions

**Big figures only matter psychologically.** They have real mechanical effects. Option barriers at big figures force dealers to hedge dynamically, creating measurable magnetism documented in academic research.

**Every big figure is equally important.** Double zero levels (1.1000, 1.2000) matter far more than intermediate handles because they attract larger option notionals.

**The convention is universal.** In EM pairs with large numbers (USD/KRW at 1,350), the "big figure" concept breaks down. Traders use percentage moves or specific levels.
