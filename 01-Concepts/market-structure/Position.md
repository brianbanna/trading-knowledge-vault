# Position

## Definition
A position is your current holding in an instrument. "[[Long]] 10M EUR/USD" means you own 10M euros against US dollars. "[[Short]] 500 lots of Brent" means you sold 500 [[Futures Contract]]s and owe delivery or must buy them back. A position has 3 attributes: instrument, direction ([[Long]] or [[Short]]), and size ([[Notional]] or contracts). No position = [[Flat]]. The position is what generates [[PnL]] as prices move.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)
Everything in trading starts and ends with position management. The position drives exposure, [[Margin]] requirements, and [[Mark to Market]] [[PnL]]. In FX, a desk carries positions across dozens of [[Currency Pair]]s and aggregate book determines risk. In commodities, a physical trader's position includes futures hedges and physical inventory. Knowing your exact position at all times is the single most important discipline in trading.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** You build a [[Long]] position in copper on the LME: 100 lots (2,500 tonnes) at 8,500 USD/tonne. Over 2 weeks, copper rallies to 8,800. [[PnL]] = 2,500 × 300 = 750,000 USD [[Unrealized PnL]]. You close 50 lots at 8,800 to lock 375,000 USD [[Realized PnL]] and let 50 lots run. Adverse case: instead of rallying, copper drops to 8,200 on China demand fears. [[Unrealized PnL]] = -750,000 USD. Risk forces a 50% cut. You sell 50 at 8,200, converting 375,000 USD paper loss to [[Realized PnL]]. The remaining 50 lots keep showing unrealized losses.

**Simplified:** What you currently own (or owe). Direction (long/short), instrument, size. PnL is just position × price change. Manage the position and the rest follows.

## Key mechanics and formulas
- Position value = Number of units × Current price
- Position PnL = Number of units × (Current price minus Entry price)
  - [[Long]]: positive PnL when price rises
  - [[Short]]: positive PnL when price falls
- Net position = Sum of all [[Long]] minus all [[Short]] in the same instrument
- Gross position = Sum of absolute values (total exposure)

## Related concepts (learn next)
- [[Flat]], the state of no position
- [[PnL]], the profit or loss generated by the position
- [[Mark to Market]], daily revaluation of the position
- [[Margin]], collateral required to maintain it
- [[Leverage]], the ratio between position size and capital
- [[Risk Management]], sizing and hedging discipline
- [[Book]], aggregate of all positions managed by a trader or desk

## Common misconceptions
- **A position is not a trade.** A trade is an event (buying or selling). A position is a state. Multiple trades can build, reduce, or flip a position.
- **Net flat is not risk free.** You can be net [[Flat]] EUR/USD but [[Long]] 100M spot offset by [[Short]] 100M [[Forward Contract]]. Net zero, but [[Basis Risk]] and time risk remain.
- **Position size and risk are not the same.** A 10M [[Position]] in EUR/USD (daily vol ~0.5%) carries far less risk than 10M in USD/TRY (daily vol ~2%).
