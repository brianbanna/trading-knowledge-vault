# Refinery Utilization

## Definition

Refinery utilization is the percentage of a refinery's or a country's total crude oil processing capacity that is actually being used at a given time. It is calculated as actual crude throughput divided by operable capacity. In the US, total operable capacity is approximately 18 million barrels per day. Utilization typically runs between 85% and 95%, with seasonal peaks in summer (gasoline season) and winter (heating oil season), and troughs during [[Turnaround Season]] (spring and fall maintenance). The [[EIA Report]] publishes weekly US refinery utilization data, making it one of the most closely watched operational metrics in energy.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Refinery utilization is the link between crude oil demand and refined product supply. High utilization (above 92%) means refiners are running hard, pulling crude out of storage, which is bullish for crude and typically bearish for product inventories. Low utilization (below 87%) means weaker crude demand and building crude inventories. [[Crack Spread]] levels drive refinery utilization decisions: when cracks are wide, refiners maximize throughput. When cracks narrow, they reduce runs. Understanding the seasonal cycle of utilization helps traders anticipate [[Calendar Spread]] moves, inventory trends, and product price dynamics.

## Concrete example

In June 2025, US refinery utilization hits 95.2%, near the operational maximum, as summer driving season demand peaks. Crude draws are running at 3 million barrels per week, supporting a backwardated WTI curve. A trader goes long the [[Gasoline Crack]] expecting refinery demand for crude to remain strong. If utilization stays above 94% through August, gasoline cracks widen to $35/bbl and the position profits. If an unexpected hurricane forces Gulf Coast refineries offline, utilization drops to 88%, gasoline cracks initially spike (less product output) but then collapse as demand fears set in, creating complex dynamics.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Utilization rate:** `Utilization = Crude Throughput / Operable Capacity × 100%`

**Seasonal pattern (US):**
- Q1 (Jan to Mar): 85 to 90% (winter/early turnaround)
- Q2 (Apr to Jun): 88 to 93% (ramp up to summer)
- Q3 (Jul to Sep): 92 to 96% (peak summer driving)
- Q4 (Oct to Dec): 87 to 92% (fall turnaround, then winter ramp)

**Crude demand impact:** Each 1% change in US utilization equals approximately 180,000 barrels per day of crude demand change.

## Common misconceptions

**"100% utilization is possible."** In practice, maximum sustained utilization is around 96 to 97% due to unplanned maintenance, blending constraints, and quality issues. Nameplate capacity includes units that may be offline.

**"Low utilization is always bearish for crude."** During turnaround season, low utilization is expected and priced in. It is only bearish when utilization drops unexpectedly outside of maintenance windows.

**"Utilization is the same everywhere."** Regional differences matter. US Gulf Coast utilization may be at 95% while Midwest refineries run at 88% due to different crude supply dynamics and seasonal patterns.
