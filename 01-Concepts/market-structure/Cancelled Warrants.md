# Cancelled Warrants

## Definition

Cancelled warrants are [[LME]] warrants submitted for withdrawal by their holders, signaling that metal will be physically removed from the LME warehouse system. The metal stays in the warehouse until collected but is no longer available for delivery against LME contracts. The cancelled warrant figure, reported daily as part of [[LME Warehouse Stocks]], leads physical demand because it reveals buyer intent before stocks actually fall. The cancellation ratio (cancelled / total stocks) indicates how tight deliverable supply is.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Rising cancelled warrants are one of the most bullish short term signals in base metals. They indicate physical buyers (fabricators, manufacturers, regional arb traders) are actively pulling metal from LME. A high cancellation ratio (above 30 to 40%) means a meaningful share of stocks is spoken for, shrinking deliverable metal and raising squeeze risk for shorts. Combined with the [[Cash to 3 Month Spread]], cancelled warrants give a real time picture of physical conditions that financial data alone misses.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** LME copper total stocks 100,000 tonnes. Cancelled warrants rise from 15,000 (15%) to 50,000 (50%) over 2 weeks. Half of LME copper is earmarked for withdrawal, leaving 50,000 tonnes deliverable. [[Cash to 3 Month Spread]] moves from $20 contango to $80 backwardation. A trader long Cash/3M at $20 contango profits $100/tonne as the spread swings to $80 backwardation. If cancellations had reversed (re warranting), the spread would have stayed in contango.

**Simplified:** A warrant is the document that says metal in an LME warehouse is available for delivery. Cancel a warrant and you flag that you intend to take the metal out. When lots of warrants get cancelled, the pool of metal anyone can claim through the exchange shrinks fast. That tightness shows up in the spread between immediate delivery and 3 month forward delivery, which inverts as buyers compete for what is left.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Cancellation ratio:** `Cancellation Ratio = Cancelled Warrants / Total Stocks × 100%`

**Interpretation:**
- Below 15%: normal, comfortable deliverable supply
- 15 to 30%: moderate demand, worth watching
- 30 to 50%: strong physical demand, potential backwardation
- Above 50%: very tight, squeeze risk elevated

**Signal sequence:**
1. Cancelled warrants rise (leading)
2. Stock draws follow (confirming)
3. Cash/3M backwardation widens (price response)
4. Flat price rises (lagging)

## Common misconceptions

**"Cancelled warrants always mean genuine demand."** Sometimes cancellations are strategic (to influence the spread or reposition between warehouses). Not all result in metal leaving LME.

**"High cancellations guarantee a rally."** If driven by arb flows (LME to SHFE) rather than end user demand, the macro impact may be limited.

**"Cancellations are irreversible."** Warrants can be re warranted if the holder changes plans. Watch both cancellations and re warrantings.
