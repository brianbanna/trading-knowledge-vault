# Cancelled Warrants

## Definition

Cancelled warrants are [[LME]] warrants that have been submitted for withdrawal by their holders, signaling that the metal will be physically removed from the LME warehouse system. When a warrant is cancelled, the metal remains in the warehouse until physically collected, but it is no longer available for delivery against LME contracts. The cancelled warrant figure, reported daily as part of [[LME Warehouse Stocks]], is a leading indicator of physical demand because it reveals buyer intentions before the actual stock reduction occurs. The cancellation ratio (cancelled warrants divided by total stocks) indicates how tight the deliverable supply is.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Rising cancelled warrants are one of the most bullish short term signals in base metals. They indicate that physical buyers (typically fabricators, manufacturers, or traders arbitraging metal to other regions) are actively pulling metal from the LME system. A high cancellation ratio (above 30 to 40%) means a significant portion of warehouse stocks are spoken for, reducing the pool of deliverable metal and increasing squeeze risk for short position holders. Combined with the [[Cash to 3 Month Spread]], cancelled warrants provide a real time picture of physical market conditions that financial data alone cannot capture.

## Concrete example

LME copper total stocks are 100,000 tonnes. Cancelled warrants rise from 15,000 tonnes (15% cancellation ratio) to 50,000 tonnes (50%) over 2 weeks. This means half of all LME copper is earmarked for withdrawal, leaving only 50,000 tonnes available for delivery. The [[Cash to 3 Month Spread]] moves from $20 contango to $80 backwardation. A trader who went long the Cash/3M spread at $20 contango profits $100/tonne as the spread swings to $80 backwardation. If cancellations had been reversed (re warranting metal back into the system), the spread would have stayed in contango.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Cancellation ratio:** `Cancellation Ratio = Cancelled Warrants / Total Stocks × 100%`

**Interpretation:**
- Below 15%: normal, comfortable deliverable supply
- 15 to 30%: moderate demand, worth watching
- 30 to 50%: strong physical demand, potential backwardation
- Above 50%: very tight, squeeze risk elevated

**Signal sequence:**
1. Cancelled warrants rise (leading indicator)
2. Actual stock draws follow (confirming indicator)
3. Cash/3M backwardation widens (price response)
4. Flat price rises (lagging response)

## Common misconceptions

**"Cancelled warrants always mean genuine demand."** Sometimes cancellations are strategic (to influence the spread or to reposition metal between warehouses). Not all cancellations result in metal leaving the LME system.

**"High cancellations guarantee a price rally."** If cancellations are driven by arb flows (metal moving from LME to SHFE) rather than end user demand, the macro impact may be limited.

**"Cancellations are irreversible."** Warrants can be re-warranted (put back on warrant) if the holder changes plans. Watching both cancellations and re-warrantings is important.
