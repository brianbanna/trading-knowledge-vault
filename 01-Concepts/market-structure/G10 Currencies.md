# G10 Currencies

## Definition
The G10 currencies are the 10 most heavily traded and liquid currencies in the world: USD, EUR, JPY, GBP, CHF, AUD, CAD, NZD, NOK, SEK. Despite the name, the group does not correspond to the G10 group of nations; it is an FX market convention. These currencies share key traits: free floating (or managed floats), deep spot and derivatives markets, minimal capital controls, and credible central banks. The 45 G10 [[Currency Pair|pairs]] account for over 80% of the ~$7.5 trillion daily global FX market.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)
G10 currencies are the default universe for most systematic FX strategies including [[Carry Trade|carry]], momentum, and value. Costs are lowest in G10, suitable for higher frequency and leveraged strategies. Several G10 currencies have strong commodity linkages: AUD (iron ore, coal), CAD (crude oil), NOK (North Sea crude), NZD (dairy, agriculture). G10 FX is a natural overlay for commodity traders managing currency exposure or expressing commodity views through currency proxies.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Macro fund constructs a G10 carry portfolio: long AUD, NZD, NOK (higher yielding) vs short CHF, JPY, EUR (lower yielding). RBA holds rates at 4.35% while BoJ remains at 0.10%; AUD/JPY carry ~4.25% annualized. The portfolio earns positive carry in calm markets. During August 2024's volatility spike, JPY surged as [[Carry Unwind|carries unwound]], and AUD/JPY fell from 105 to 94 in days, wiping out months of carry income. G10 carry is accessible and liquid but still vulnerable to sharp reversals.

**Simplified:** G10 is the standard list of major developed market currencies that FX desks trade most actively. Big liquid market, tight spreads, fair amount of policy stability. Most quant FX strategies are built around G10 pairs because the data is clean and the costs are low. Some G10 currencies move with commodities (AUD, CAD, NOK), some act as safe havens (JPY, CHF), giving you a range of macro themes within the same liquid universe.

## Key mechanics and formulas
- **The 10 currencies**: USD, EUR, JPY, GBP, CHF, AUD, CAD, NZD, NOK, SEK.
- **Number of unique pairs**: 10 × 9 / 2 = 45 pairs.
- **Liquidity hierarchy** (by daily volume): EUR/USD > USD/JPY > GBP/USD > AUD/USD > USD/CAD > USD/CHF > NZD/USD > EUR/GBP > others.
- **Commodity linked G10**: AUD (iron ore, coal), CAD (crude oil, natural gas), NOK (Brent crude), NZD (dairy, soft commodities).
- **Funding currencies**: JPY, CHF, EUR (low or negative rates historically).

## Common misconceptions

**G10 currencies are risk free.** GBP fell over 8% on the Brexit referendum night in June 2016. CHF surged 30% intraday when the SNB removed its EUR/CHF floor in January 2015.

**G10 means the G10 nations.** The FX G10 list does not match any official political grouping. Norway and Sweden are in FX G10 but not in the G7 or political G10.

**All G10 pairs are equally liquid.** EUR/USD trades ~$2 trillion daily; NOK/SEK trades a few billion. Liquidity varies enormously within G10.
