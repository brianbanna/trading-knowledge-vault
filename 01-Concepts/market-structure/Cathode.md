# Cathode

## Definition

Cathode is the final refined form of copper tradeable on the [[LME]]. LME Grade A cathode must be at least 99.99% pure copper, in large flat sheets (100 to 125 kg each). It comes from pyrometallurgy (smelting/refining of [[Concentrate]]) or hydrometallurgy (SX EW, solvent extraction electrowinning). Only LME approved brands (producers who passed quality and financial audits) deliver against LME contracts via the [[Warrant]] system. The cathode anchors the entire copper futures pricing chain.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Cathode premiums above the LME benchmark reflect the real delivered cost in specific markets. The premium includes freight, import duties, insurance, and local supply/demand. Physical buyer total cost: LME price + cathode premium. High premiums ($100 to $200/tonne above LME) signal strong local demand or tight supply, even when LME is stable. Different regions have different benchmark premiums: US Midwest, European CIF, Shanghai bonded, Japan CIF. Tracking regional premiums reveals where copper is tight and where arb flows redirect supply.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** LME copper at $9,500/tonne. Shanghai bonded premium $120/tonne, US Midwest premium $40/tonne. The $80/tonne differential (after freight) makes shipping cathode to China more profitable than to the US. A trader buys cathode in Europe (LME + $50 European premium), ships to Shanghai (freight $40/tonne), sells at LME + $120 Shanghai premium. Net $30/tonne before financing. If Chinese demand weakens and the Shanghai premium drops to $50, the trade becomes uneconomic.

**Simplified:** Cathode is the finished, pure copper product traded on the LME. The benchmark LME price is just the starting point. To actually buy physical copper somewhere in the world, you pay the LME price plus a regional premium that reflects local tightness and shipping costs. When premiums diverge enough across regions, traders ship metal from cheap regions to expensive ones until premiums equalize.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Delivered copper cost:** `Total Cost = LME Price + Regional Premium + Freight + Insurance + Duties`

**LME Grade A specifications:**
- Purity: ≥ 99.99% Cu
- Form: cathode sheets
- Weight: 100 to 125 kg each
- Must be from LME registered brand

**Key regional premiums (indicative):**
- US Midwest: $30 to $100/tonne
- European CIF: $30 to $80/tonne
- Shanghai bonded: $50 to $200/tonne
- Japan CIF: $50 to $120/tonne

## Common misconceptions

**"LME copper price is what you pay for physical."** You always pay LME plus a premium, which can be a meaningful share of total cost.

**"All cathode is the same."** Only LME approved brands deliver against LME contracts. Non approved brands trade at discounts. SX EW vs smelted cathode can have different trace element profiles.

**"Premiums are stable."** They spike sharply during logistics disruptions, trade policy changes, or sudden demand surges.
