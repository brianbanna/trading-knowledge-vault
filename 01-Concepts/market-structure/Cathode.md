# Cathode

## Definition

Cathode is the final refined form of copper that is tradeable on the [[LME]]. LME Grade A copper cathode must be at least 99.99% pure copper, in the form of large flat sheets (typically 100 to 125 kg each). It is produced through either pyrometallurgical (smelting/refining of [[Concentrate]]) or hydrometallurgical (SX EW, solvent extraction electrowinning) processes. Only cathode from LME approved brands (producers who have passed quality and financial audits) can be delivered against LME contracts via the [[Warrant]] system. The cathode is the physical product that anchors the entire copper futures pricing chain.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Cathode premiums above the LME benchmark price reflect the real delivered cost of copper in specific markets. The premium includes freight, import duties, insurance, and local supply/demand conditions. In physical copper trading, the total cost to a buyer is: LME price + cathode premium. When premiums are high ($100 to $200/tonne above LME), it signals strong local demand or tight physical supply, even if the LME price itself is stable. Different regions have different benchmark premiums: US Midwest, European CIF, Shanghai bonded, Japan CIF. Tracking premiums across regions reveals where copper is tight and where arb flows may redirect supply.

## Concrete example

LME copper is at $9,500/tonne. The Shanghai bonded premium is $120/tonne while the US Midwest premium is only $40/tonne. This $80/tonne differential (after freight adjustment) makes it more profitable to ship cathode to China than to the US. A physical trader buys cathode in Europe (at LME + $50 European premium), ships to Shanghai (freight $40/tonne), and sells at LME + $120 Shanghai premium. Net profit: $30/tonne before financing. If Chinese demand weakens and the Shanghai premium drops to $50, the trade becomes uneconomic.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Delivered copper cost:** `Total Cost = LME Price + Regional Premium + Freight + Insurance + Duties`

**LME Grade A specifications:**
- Purity: ≥ 99.99% Cu
- Form: cathode sheets
- Weight: individual cathodes 100 to 125 kg
- Must be from LME registered brand

**Key regional premiums (indicative ranges):**
- US Midwest: $30 to $100/tonne
- European CIF: $30 to $80/tonne
- Shanghai bonded: $50 to $200/tonne
- Japan CIF: $50 to $120/tonne

## Common misconceptions

**"LME copper price is what you pay for physical copper."** You always pay LME plus a premium. The premium can be a significant percentage of total cost.

**"All copper cathode is the same."** Only LME approved brands are deliverable against LME contracts. Non approved brands may trade at discounts. SX EW cathode vs smelted cathode can have different trace element profiles.

**"Premiums are stable and predictable."** They can spike sharply during logistics disruptions, trade policy changes, or sudden demand surges.
