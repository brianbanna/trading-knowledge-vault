# TC RC

## Definition

TC/RC stands for Treatment Charge and Refining Charge, the fees paid by mining companies to smelters for converting [[Concentrate]] (raw ore) into refined metal (such as [[Cathode|copper cathode]]). The TC is charged per dry metric tonne of concentrate, and the RC is charged per pound of contained metal. Together they represent the smelter's revenue for providing processing services. TC/RCs are negotiated annually between miners and smelters (the annual benchmark) and also trade on a spot basis. They are the key indicator of the balance between concentrate supply (from mines) and smelting capacity (processing demand).

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

TC/RCs tell you which side of the concentrate market has bargaining power. When TC/RCs are high, smelters are in the driver's seat because concentrate supply is abundant and miners compete for limited smelting capacity. When TC/RCs are low, miners have power because concentrate is scarce and smelters compete for feed. This dynamic directly affects refined metal supply: if TC/RCs collapse and smelters become unprofitable, they curtail operations, reducing refined output and eventually tightening the refined metal market. For copper specifically, the TC/RC cycle is one of the most important medium term fundamental indicators.

## Concrete example

The 2026 annual copper TC/RC benchmark is set at $80/tonne and $0.08/lb (TC $80, RC 8 cents). A large Chilean mine suffers a disruption, reducing concentrate supply. Spot TC/RCs drop to $30/5 as smelters compete for scarce concentrate. Chinese smelters earning only $30/5 are below breakeven ($50/5 typically) and begin curtailing production. Reduced smelter output tightens the refined copper market, and LME copper rises $400/tonne over 3 months. A trader who tracked the TC/RC collapse as a leading indicator went long copper early.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**TC/RC convention:** TC = $/dry metric tonne of concentrate. RC = cents/lb of payable metal.

**Example:** TC $80/DMT, RC 8.0 ¢/lb for copper concentrate with 25% copper content.

**Smelter revenue per tonne of concentrate:**
`Revenue ≈ (Contained Metal × LME Price × Payable %) - (Contained Metal × RC) + TC`

**Breakeven TC/RC:** Varies by smelter but typically $40 to $60 TC / 4 to 6 ¢/lb RC for Chinese copper smelters.

**Annual benchmark:** Set in Q4 each year for the following calendar year, negotiated between major miners (Freeport, BHP, Codelco) and smelters (Jiangxi Copper, Aurubis).

## Common misconceptions

**"Low TC/RCs are bearish for copper."** Low TC/RCs mean concentrate is scarce, which eventually means less refined copper output, which is bullish for refined copper prices.

**"TC/RCs only matter for mining and smelting companies."** They are a critical fundamental indicator for any copper trader because they forecast refined supply changes 3 to 6 months ahead.

**"The annual benchmark is the price everyone pays."** Many contracts are priced off spot TC/RCs, which can diverge significantly from the annual benchmark during the year.
