# Dated Brent

## Definition

Dated Brent is the spot price assessment for physical North Sea crude (the BFOET basket: Brent, Forties, Oseberg, Ekofisk, Troll) with a specific delivery window, 10 to 25 days forward. Assessed daily by S&P Global Platts through the [[Platts Window]] (Market on Close process). Dated Brent is the foundational physical price in global oil: the cash price anchoring the Brent futures complex to physical reality. The spread between Dated Brent and ICE Brent front month is the CFD (Contract for Differences), capturing the short term physical/paper relationship.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Dated Brent is the price that matters for physical oil. ICE Brent futures provide liquidity and price discovery; Dated Brent sets the actual cost of buying or selling a physical North Sea cargo. Roughly 2/3 of global crude is priced directly or indirectly off Dated Brent through formulas and differentials. The Dated/futures spread ([[CFD Crude]]) is one of the most actively traded short term indicators. When Dated Brent trades premium to futures (strong physical market), it signals near term tightness that futures may not yet reflect.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Physical trading house is offered a Forties cargo loading in 15 days. Platts Dated Brent is $83.20/bbl. ICE Brent front month is $82.50. [[CFD Crude|CFD]] = $0.70 (Dated premium). Trader buys the cargo at Dated Brent flat ($83.20) and sells ICE Brent futures at $82.50, locking in a $0.70/bbl cost expected to be recovered through the offtake contract. If physical demand strengthens and the CFD widens to $1.50, the cargo appreciates vs the hedge. If physical softens and the CFD flips to −$0.30, the cargo loses value.

**Simplified:** Brent futures are the financial price. Dated Brent is the physical price for cargoes loading in the next few weeks. They are linked but not identical. The gap between them (the CFD) tells you whether the actual barrels are tight or loose right now. A wide positive gap means physical buyers are bidding for prompt cargoes. A negative gap means there are too many barrels around. Physical traders use Dated Brent as the anchor for almost every crude formula in the world.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Dated Brent assessment:** Set daily by Platts based on Platts Window trading in BFOET cargoes (600,000 barrel partials) loading 10 to 25 days forward.

**CFD:** `CFD = Dated Brent − ICE Brent Front Month Futures`

**Dated to front month (DFL):** Same as CFD. Positive = physical premium. Negative = physical discount.

**BFOET basket:** 5 grades (Brent, Forties, Oseberg, Ekofisk, Troll) assessed; cheapest to deliver sets the Dated Brent level.

## Common misconceptions

**"Brent futures and Dated Brent are the same."** Related but distinct. Futures are financial with standardized expiry. Dated Brent is physical with a specific delivery window. The CFD between them fluctuates significantly.

**"Dated Brent is set by supply and demand alone."** The assessment is based on Platts Window activity, a specific 30 minute period. Strategic trading in the window can influence the assessment, drawing regulatory scrutiny.

**"Dated Brent is a single grade."** It is a 5 grade basket. Cheapest to deliver mechanics mean changes in relative supply of individual grades can shift which one sets Dated Brent.
