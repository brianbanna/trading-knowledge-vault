# Gold Silver Ratio

## Definition

The gold/silver ratio (GSR) is simply the price of gold divided by the price of silver, telling you how many ounces of silver it takes to buy one ounce of gold. It is one of the oldest relative value metrics in finance, tracked for centuries. Over the past 50 years, the ratio has ranged from approximately 30 (in 1980 and 2011, when silver was relatively expensive) to over 120 (in March 2020, during peak COVID panic). The long term average since 1970 is approximately 65 to 70. The ratio tends to be mean reverting over multi year horizons but can trend persistently for years in between.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

The GSR is the primary relative value trade in precious metals. When the ratio exceeds 80, silver is historically cheap relative to gold and a long silver/short gold position has favorable odds over a 6 to 18 month horizon. Below 60, silver is relatively expensive. The ratio reflects the market's relative valuation of silver's industrial demand (which benefits from economic growth) vs gold's safe haven and investment demand (which benefits from fear and real rate declines). Extreme GSR readings are a contrarian signal: very high ratios (above 85) often occur during risk off panics when silver's industrial demand suffers.

## Concrete example

In March 2020, the GSR spikes to 125 (gold at $1,680, silver at $13.45). A trader goes long silver and short gold, betting on mean reversion. Over the next 15 months, silver rallies to $28 while gold reaches $1,900. The GSR drops to 68. On a ratio neutral trade (equal dollar amounts), the silver leg gained 108% while the gold leg lost 13%. Net profit is substantial. If instead a prolonged recession had killed industrial demand for silver while gold continued to benefit from monetary easing, the ratio could have stayed elevated.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Gold/Silver Ratio:** `GSR = Gold Price ($/oz) / Silver Price ($/oz)`

**Historical ranges:**
- Extreme high: 120+ (only during severe crises)
- High: 80 to 100 (silver cheap, typically during risk off)
- Average: 65 to 75
- Low: 40 to 60 (silver expensive, typically during commodity booms)
- Extreme low: 30 to 40 (only during silver mania)

**Trade expression:** Long silver / short gold, ratio neutral (equal dollar exposure on each leg).

## Common misconceptions

**"The ratio always mean reverts quickly."** It can trend for years. The ratio was above 80 for most of 2019 to 2020 before finally correcting.

**"A high ratio means buy silver outright."** The ratio trade is a relative value trade (long silver, short gold). Buying silver outright without hedging with gold exposes you to overall precious metals direction.

**"There is a natural or fundamental ratio."** The geological ratio of silver to gold in the earth's crust is approximately 17:1, but this has no bearing on market pricing. The tradeable ratio reflects relative supply/demand and investor sentiment.
