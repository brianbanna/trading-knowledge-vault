# Gold Silver Ratio

## Definition

The gold/silver ratio (GSR) is gold price divided by silver price, expressing how many ounces of silver buy 1 ounce of gold. One of the oldest relative value metrics in finance, tracked for centuries. Over the past 50 years the ratio has ranged from ~30 (1980, 2011, when silver was relatively expensive) to over 120 (March 2020, peak COVID panic). Long term average since 1970 is ~65 to 70. Mean reverting over multi year horizons but trends persistently for years between turns.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

The GSR is the primary relative value trade in precious metals. Above 80, silver is historically cheap vs gold and a long silver/short gold position has favorable odds over a 6 to 18 month horizon. Below 60, silver is relatively expensive. The ratio reflects the market's relative pricing of silver's industrial demand (sensitive to growth) vs gold's safe haven and investment demand (sensitive to fear and real rates). Extreme readings are contrarian signals: very high ratios (above 85) appear in risk off panics when silver's industrial demand suffers.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** March 2020. GSR spikes to 125 (gold $1,680, silver $13.45). Trader goes long silver and short gold, betting on reversion. Over 15 months silver rallies to $28 while gold reaches $1,900. GSR drops to 68. On a ratio neutral trade (equal dollar amounts), the silver leg gained 108% and the gold leg lost 13%. Net is substantial. If a prolonged recession had killed industrial silver demand while gold benefited from monetary easing, the ratio could have stayed elevated for years.

**Simplified:** Gold is the safe haven metal. Silver is half safe haven, half industrial. When the world panics, both rise, but silver gets dumped because factories slow down. That pushes the gold/silver ratio higher. When the economy recovers, silver catches up and the ratio drops. Trading the ratio means buying the cheap metal and shorting the expensive one, betting they converge. It is slow, multi year work, not a fast trade.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Gold/Silver Ratio:** `GSR = Gold Price ($/oz) / Silver Price ($/oz)`

**Historical ranges:**
- Extreme high: 120+ (only during severe crises)
- High: 80 to 100 (silver cheap, risk off)
- Average: 65 to 75
- Low: 40 to 60 (silver expensive, commodity booms)
- Extreme low: 30 to 40 (silver mania)

**Trade expression:** Long silver / short gold, ratio neutral (equal dollar exposure each leg).

## Common misconceptions

**"The ratio always reverts quickly."** It can trend for years. The ratio was above 80 for most of 2019 to 2020 before finally correcting.

**"A high ratio means buy silver outright."** The ratio trade is relative value (long silver, short gold). Buying silver outright exposes you to overall precious metals direction.

**"There is a natural or fundamental ratio."** The geological ratio of silver to gold in the earth's crust is ~17:1, but this has no bearing on market pricing. The tradeable ratio reflects relative supply/demand and investor sentiment.
