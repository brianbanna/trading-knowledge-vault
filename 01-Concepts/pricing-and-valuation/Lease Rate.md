# Lease Rate

## Definition

The gold lease rate is the interest rate charged for borrowing physical gold from a lender (central bank or bullion bank) for a specified period. Central banks earn a return on their gold reserves by lending to commercial banks, who use it for short selling, hedging, or satisfying physical delivery. The lease rate is derived from the relationship between the gold forward rate ([[GOFO]]) and the dollar rate: Lease Rate = Dollar Rate − GOFO. Spiking lease rates signal physical gold scarcity and competition for metal. Low lease rates indicate ample supply in the lending market.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Spiking lease rates signal physical scarcity and potential short squeeze conditions. A sharp rise means borrowers cannot easily find physical, indicating strong physical demand, exchange delivery stress, or a pullback in central bank lending. Lease rate spikes have historically preceded or coincided with gold rallies as physical tightness translates to higher prices. Monitoring lease rates provides an early warning for physical market stress that futures prices alone may miss.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Gold 1 month lease rates spike from 0.1% to 2.5% over a week. Trader reads this as physical scarcity (heavy ETF inflows or central bank demand). She goes long gold at $2,400. Physical tightness drives gold to $2,500 as borrowers needing metal are forced to buy outright rather than lease. Profit: $100/oz. If the lease rate spike had been caused by a temporary logistics issue (gold stuck in transit) rather than genuine scarcity, rates normalize quickly and the opportunity evaporates.

**Simplified:** If you need physical gold but do not own any, you can borrow it from a central bank or bullion bank and pay them an interest rate. That rate is the lease rate. When the rate spikes, it means everyone is fighting for the same metal. That usually happens when demand is high and supply is short. Watching the lease rate gives you a read on the physical market that the futures screen does not show.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Lease rate:** `Lease Rate = Dollar Interest Rate (SOFR) − GOFO`

**Typical gold lease rates:** 0.05% to 0.50% (normal), 1 to 3% (elevated), > 3% (extreme tightness).

**Who lends gold:** Central banks (largest lenders), bullion banks, sovereign wealth funds.

**Who borrows gold:** Banks needing physical for delivery, miners hedging forward production (historical), short sellers.

## Common misconceptions

**"Lease rates are publicly quoted like LIBOR."** Not a formal benchmark. Derived from GOFO and dollar rates or inferred from OTC transactions.

**"High lease rates always mean gold will rally."** Lease rate spikes can be caused by temporary technical factors (settlement bottlenecks) rather than genuine scarcity.

**"Central banks always lend their gold."** Some have reduced lending or repatriated reserves, shrinking the lendable pool and structurally supporting lease rates.
