# London Fix

## Definition

The London Fix (officially the LBMA Gold Price since 2015) is the benchmark gold price set twice daily through an electronic auction administered by ICE Benchmark Administration. AM fix at 10:30 London time, PM fix at 3:00 PM London time. Participants submit buy and sell orders at a proposed price; the price adjusts iteratively until supply and demand balance within a tolerance band. The resulting fix is the global reference for settling gold derivatives, valuing ETF holdings, pricing mine production contracts, and benchmarking central bank reserves. The silver fix (LBMA Silver Price) occurs once daily at noon London time.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

The London Fix is the price that matters for physical gold settlement. Billions of dollars in contracts reference the PM fix specifically. Gold ETFs (GLD, IAU) value holdings at the PM fix. Central banks mark reserves to the fix. Miners sell output on fix related formulas. Because so much value settles at the fix, concentrated order flows around the auction create predictable short term patterns. Academic research documents systematic price patterns around the fix (the "fixing anomaly"). The 2015 transition from telephone based fixing to electronic auction was driven partly by manipulation concerns.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** A gold ETF needs to redeem 50,000 ounces and settles at the PM fix. The ETF's authorized participant (a bank) must sell physical at the fix price. Anticipating selling pressure, the bank pre hedges by selling futures before the fix, creating downward pressure. A trader monitoring ETF flow sees the large pending redemption and goes short gold ahead of the 3 PM fix. If the fix settles $3/oz below pre fix on the concentrated selling, she covers and profits. If unexpected buying overwhelms the ETF selling, the fix settles higher and the short loses.

**Simplified:** Twice a day in London, an auction sets the official gold price. Everyone who has to settle a gold contract uses that price. Because trillions of value reference it, the order flow concentrates around the auction. Knowing who needs to buy or sell at the fix lets you front run or fade the move. It is the gold market's daily liquidity event.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Auction schedule:**
- AM Fix: 10:30 AM London time
- PM Fix: 3:00 PM London time

**Auction process:**
1. ICE proposes an opening price
2. Participants submit buy/sell orders
3. If imbalance exceeds tolerance (10,000 oz), price adjusts
4. Repeat until balance is achieved
5. Final price published as LBMA Gold Price

**Key references:**
- Gold ETFs (GLD, IAU): NAV at PM fix
- Central bank reserves: marked at fix prices
- Mine hedging contracts: often reference monthly average of daily fixes

## Common misconceptions

**"The fix is just the spot price at that time."** The fix is a separate auction that can settle at a price different from the prevailing spot market. The auction concentrates liquidity and produces a single settlement price.

**"The fix cannot be influenced."** The 2015 reform improved transparency, but concentrated order flow inherently creates price impact. Large participants influence the auction outcome.

**"Only the PM fix matters."** The AM fix is used for certain contracts and serves as a reference for Asian/European trading. Both fixes matter.
