# Troy Ounce

## Definition

A troy ounce is the standard unit for precious metals, equal to 31.1035 grams. Distinct from the avoirdupois ounce used for everyday weight (28.3495 grams). The troy system dates to the medieval trading fairs in Troyes, France. All precious metals (gold, silver, platinum, palladium) are quoted, traded, and weighed in troy ounces globally. A London Good Delivery gold bar weighs approximately 400 troy ounces (12.4 kg). A COMEX gold futures contract covers 100 troy ounces. Confusing troy with standard ounces is a 9.7% error that compounds across large positions.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Every precious metals calculation uses troy ounces. Converting between grams, kilograms, and troy ounces is a daily necessity. Comparing prices across markets (COMEX in $/troy oz vs Shanghai Gold Exchange in CNY/gram) requires the conversion. Unit errors lead to significant mispricing, especially in physical trading where tonnage (metric tonnes) must be converted to troy ounces for settlement.

## Concrete example

**Concrete:** Trader evaluates Shanghai Gold Exchange at CNY 580/gram versus LBMA at $2,500/troy oz. Convert: $2,500 / 31.1035 = $80.38/gram. At 7.15 CNY/USD, that is CNY 574.70/gram. Shanghai premium = CNY 580 − 574.70 = CNY 5.30/gram, or about $0.74/gram ($23/troy oz). Premium signals Chinese demand strength, suggests arbitrage opportunity into China. Using avoirdupois ounces (28.35g) instead would put the calculation off by 9.7%, falsely showing a CNY 25/gram premium and triggering a mistaken trade.

**Simplified:** Gold and silver use their own unit. A troy ounce is 31.1 grams, a regular ounce is 28.3 grams. Almost 10% difference. Every precious metals price you see is per troy ounce. Mix them up and you misprice everything by 10%. Always convert through grams to compare quotes across exchanges that use different units.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Conversions:**
- 1 troy ounce = 31.1035 grams
- 1 kilogram = 32.1507 troy ounces
- 1 metric tonne = 32,150.7 troy ounces
- 1 troy ounce = 1.0971 avoirdupois ounces

**Standard bar weights:**
- London Good Delivery gold bar: ~400 troy ounces (12.4 kg)
- COMEX gold contract: 100 troy ounces
- COMEX silver contract: 5,000 troy ounces

**Price per gram from price per troy ounce:** `$/gram = $/troy oz ÷ 31.1035`

## Common misconceptions

**"A troy ounce and a regular ounce are the same."** A troy ounce is 9.7% heavier than a standard (avoirdupois) ounce. Significant difference in large transactions.

**"Troy ounces are only for gold."** All precious metals (silver, platinum, palladium) use troy ounces.

**"Metric conversion eliminates troy ounces."** Despite the metric system, troy remains the universal precious metals standard. Shanghai Gold Exchange quotes in grams, but international markets overwhelmingly use troy ounces.
