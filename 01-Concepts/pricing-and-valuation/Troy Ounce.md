# Troy Ounce

## Definition

A troy ounce is the standard unit of measurement for precious metals, equal to 31.1035 grams. It is distinct from the more common avoirdupois ounce used for everyday weight measurement (28.3495 grams). The troy system dates back to the medieval trading fairs in Troyes, France. All precious metals (gold, silver, platinum, palladium) are quoted, traded, and weighed in troy ounces globally. A standard London Good Delivery gold bar weighs approximately 400 troy ounces (12.4 kg), while a COMEX gold futures contract covers 100 troy ounces. Confusing troy ounces with standard ounces is a 9.7% error that compounds across large positions.

## Why it matters (commodities and FX)

Every precious metals calculation must use troy ounces. Converting between grams, kilograms, and troy ounces is a daily necessity for metals traders. When comparing gold prices across markets (COMEX in $/troy oz vs Shanghai Gold Exchange in CNY/gram), the troy ounce conversion is essential. Errors in unit conversion can lead to significant mispricing, especially in physical trading where tonnage (metric tonnes) must be converted to troy ounces for financial settlement.

## Concrete example

A trader is evaluating the Shanghai Gold Exchange price of CNY 580/gram against the LBMA price of $2,500/troy ounce. To compare: $2,500 / 31.1035 grams/troy oz = $80.38/gram. At an exchange rate of 7.15 CNY/USD, this equals CNY 574.70/gram. The Shanghai premium is CNY 580 minus CNY 574.70 = CNY 5.30/gram, or approximately $0.74/gram ($23/troy ounce). This premium signals Chinese demand strength. If the trader had used avoirdupois ounces (28.35g) instead of troy ounces (31.10g), the calculation would be off by 9.7%.

## Key mechanics and formulas

**Conversions:**
- 1 troy ounce = 31.1035 grams
- 1 kilogram = 32.1507 troy ounces
- 1 metric tonne = 32,150.7 troy ounces
- 1 troy ounce = 1.0971 avoirdupois ounces

**Standard bar weights:**
- London Good Delivery gold bar: ~400 troy ounces (12.4 kg)
- COMEX gold contract: 100 troy ounces
- COMEX silver contract: 5,000 troy ounces

**Price per gram from price per troy ounce:** `$/gram = $/troy oz ÷ 31.1035`

## Common misconceptions

**"A troy ounce and a regular ounce are the same."** A troy ounce is 9.7% heavier than a standard (avoirdupois) ounce. This is a significant difference in large transactions.

**"Troy ounces are only used for gold."** All precious metals, including silver, platinum, and palladium, use troy ounces as the standard unit.

**"Metric conversion eliminates troy ounces."** Despite the metric system, the troy ounce remains the universal standard for precious metals quoting. The Shanghai Gold Exchange quotes in grams, but international markets overwhelmingly use troy ounces.
